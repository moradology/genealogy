## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Starting from the existing source ledger (which already established the Genevieve Clemans FamilySearch sibling lead, the Margaret Ellen Flaherty Clemans Find a Grave memorial, the Edward J. Flaherty Martin/Kate parent lead, and the Indiana Marjorie Opal Clemans collision as a working lead), I pulled the underlying Find a Grave pages directly (via browser-UA curl, several requiring retries past Cloudflare) and cross-checked them against a Wayback Machine capture of the Douglas County, WI GenWeb Calvary Cemetery transcription, the Wisconsin Historical Society's free Pre-1907 Vital Records index, the full digitized 'Descendants of Christian Eby' genealogy text, and the St. Louis City Recorder / Archdiocese of St. Louis genealogy pages. New, directly-verified facts: Margaret Ellen Flaherty was born 21 Apr 1901 in Superior, Douglas County, Wisconsin and died 8 Mar 1986 in St. Paul, Minnesota; her parents Martin Flaherty (1867 Ireland-1934) and Kate Flaherty (1874 Ireland-1913) are buried together at Calvary Cemetery, Superior, WI (corroborated by an independent USGenWeb transcription); and a large but unspecific-county Flaherty family cluster occupies several adjoining plots there. A significant new problem surfaced: Find a Grave's own 'spouse' link on Margaret's memorial points to a 'Michael D. Clemans' (1902-1961, born Wisconsin, died St. Paul MN) rather than to 'Talmadge Dewitt Clemans,' directly contradicting the marriage-index note quoted on the same page ('CLEMANS, TALMAGE'). A chronology check also surfaced a serious, previously unflagged problem: if Marjorie Clemans is the biological daughter of the 1921 Talmadge/Margaret marriage, she would have been only ~12-13 when Evelyn Mundell was born in 1935, which is not plausible without further explanation. The Indiana Marjorie Opal Clemans exclusion was strengthened with direct quotes from a digitized 1949 Eby-family genealogy book showing a fully distinct Clemans/Johnson/Kissinger family in Liberty Mills/South Bend, Indiana. No source consulted specifies an Irish county for the Flaherty family; the WI Pre-1907 free vital-records index (despite covering many other Douglas County Flahertys) contains no Martin, Kate, or Margaret entries at all, which is itself a research clue. St. Louis's 1921 marriage record and the Archdiocese's sacramental microfilm are both real, identifiable, but not freely name-searchable online (archives request / FamilySearch browse-only, respectively) - flagged as such rather than guessed at. FamilySearch Ancestors tree pages (Genevieve Clemans, Marjorie Opal Clemans) and Ancestry's 1940-census snippet remain JS/paywall-blocked and were not treated as confirmed or denied.

### Wave 2

Starting from the predecessor's four open threads, this wave made its biggest advance on the Clemans identity/origin problem and confirmed several negative results rather than the Irish-county question, which stayed unresolved despite direct new checks. The headline finding: cross-referencing Michael D. Clemans's Find a Grave bio (parents "Rollie and Ellen (Martin) Clemans"), an Ancestry index snippet for "Talmage D Clemans" (identical birth year/state, identical parents "Roland Clemens"/"Ella C. (Ellen) Martin"), and — new this wave — the Wisconsin Historical Society's official Pre-1907 Vital Records state index, which independently confirms a birth record for "Clemans, Michael D" on the exact date 8 Sep 1902 in Barron County, Wisconsin, and a marriage record for "Clemans, Rollie C" on 23 Feb 1902 in Barron County cross-linked by the Society's own "Possible Spousal Matches" feature to a same-date bride "Martin, C Ellen." This is about as strong a non-primary corroboration chain as free sources allow, and it makes it very likely that "Michael D. Clemans" (Margaret's Find-a-Grave-linked spouse) and "Talmadge Dewitt Clemans" (FamilySearch's name for Margaret's husband) are the same man — though no single record viewed shows both given names together, so this is reported as a strengthened frame, not a closed fact. The same Wisconsin index surfaced two more siblings (Helen G. and Inez M. Clemans, born Barron Co. 1904/1906) and, via Delano Clemans's death-certificate-sourced Find a Grave bio, a concrete birthplace lead for the parent generation: father Ohio-born, mother Wisconsin-born, family later in St. Paul, MN by 1911-1916. On the Flaherty side, pulling all seven of Martin and Kate Flaherty's children's own Find a Grave memorials (including the two who died recently enough, 1989 and 1991, to typically carry obituary text) confirmed the "no Irish county anywhere" finding extends past the parents to the whole next generation; a Douglas County WI 1897 birth record for "Flaherty, Edward" is a plausible but unconfirmed candidate match since Wisconsin's free index withholds parents' names on birth records. The predecessor's "why are Martin/Kate/Margaret absent from the pre-1907 index" puzzle was partly resolved rather than deepened: the Historical Society's own collection scope statement confirms it holds no records after 30 Sep 1907, which fully explains Kate's 1913 death being absent — leaving only Margaret's own claimed 1901 Douglas County birth as a genuinely unexplained gap. On the Marjorie Clemans chronology problem, this wave could not find her birth year (FamilySearch's ancestors.familysearch.org page was confirmed by direct HTML inspection to be a pure client-side JS shell with zero embedded data) but did rule out two same-name collisions: no Find a Grave memorial exists for "Marjorie Mundell" or "Homer Mundell" matching the Colorado/Kansas family, and a same-name "Marjorie E. Mundell" obituary (1947-2014, Pennsylvania) was directly viewed and excluded. Evelyn's own obituary text shows Homer and Marjorie had at least 8 children total, which sharpens (without resolving) the implausibility that Marjorie was born immediately after the 1921 marriage. The St. Louis parish question remains open; the Missouri State Archives' free digitized county-records database, which could plausibly answer it, was blocked by a Cloudflare JS challenge and needs an interactive-browser retry rather than curl.

### Wave 3

Read index.html's Mundell/Clemans/Flaherty branch section and source-index.json (26 already-logged sources) before starting; nothing below duplicates a fact already recorded there. This wave verified several of the predecessor's headline claims directly against primary/archival pages myself (Michael D. Clemans's Find a Grave record, the Wisconsin Historical Society's Rollie C. Clemans/C. Ellen Martin spousal cross-link, Martin/Kate Flaherty's own memorials) and pushed into new territory in four directions. First, a genuinely new complication on the Flaherty Irish-origin question: Wisconsin's own pre-1907 vital-records index lists the county for Edward Flaherty's 1897 birth simply as 'Douglas,' but Find a Grave's own transcription of Edward's birthplace reads 'Douglas, Marquette County' - a real but different Wisconsin place ~300 miles from Douglas County/Superior where his parents are buried, meaning this long-standing 'candidate' record needs the actual microfilm image before it can anchor anything. Second, a statewide (not just Douglas-County) Wisconsin search for Martin Flaherty's own marriage record, and a check of the St. Louis naturalization index (surnames E-K, 1906-1928), both came back negative for Martin and Kate, sharpening rather than resolving the puzzle: they likely married either in Ireland or in an unindexed jurisdiction, and Martin did not naturalize through St. Louis. Third, on the Clemans side, Delano Clemans's own Find a Grave birthplace field (Ohio, Oct 1911) reveals the family's actual path was Wisconsin (1902) -> Ohio (1911) -> Minnesota (by 1916), not a direct Wisconsin-to-Minnesota move, and the Barron County WI Clemans marriage cluster (Mary Clemans/A. Murdock 1883; Jacob H. Clemans/Suceil Hayves 1902) alongside Rollie's own 1902 marriage is a concrete, unexploited sibling lead for finding Rollie's Ohio county via a shared census household. Fourth, on the St. Louis parish question, Margaret's Find a Grave marriage citation yields a specific archival locator (St. Louis Vol. 3, p. 237) and this wave located two free public Missouri portals that could resolve it (the city's Fidlar Vitals Web Portal and the state's County & Municipal Records Database), but both are JS/anti-bot gated against curl and need an interactive-browser retry, same as the predecessor's blocked attempt. Marjorie Clemans's birth year remains completely undocumented; the free 1950 census portal was identified as the most promising untried lead but is likewise JS-gated. No new work was needed on the already-excluded Indiana Marjorie Opal Clemans/Clay Stamper collision, which index.html already treats as resolved.
