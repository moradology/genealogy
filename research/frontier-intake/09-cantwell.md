## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Despite the genealogy.com forum's Cloudflare bot-wall, three of the eight Cantwell-thread message pages (763, 859, 909) loaded successfully with a browser User-Agent and short retries, and Find a Grave (also intermittently Cloudflare-gated but mostly penetrable with retries) supplied far more structured data than the existing index.html/source-index.json capture. Sarah Alice Cantwell (15 Jan 1864 - 3 Feb 1939) now has her own Find a Grave memorial with a full transcribed 1939 obituary: she was a native of Mount Pleasant, Henry County, Iowa, moved young to Hastings, Adams County, Nebraska, married John P. Mundell there in 1885 (obituary) / 1886 (Find a Grave family panel -- unresolved one-year conflict), had 11 children (only 5 survivors named in 1939, including a previously undocumented daughter Thelma Hileman of Farmersville, CA), and died at Glencoe, Payne County, Oklahoma, where she and her husband \"homesteaded a farm.\" She was, per her own obituary, \"the last survivor of her father's family.\" The Cantwell forum thread's 1870 census transcript places a 5-year-old \"Sarah A.\" in Henry County, Iowa in Barnabas \"Barney\" Cantwell's household -- consistent with her birth year and birthplace. Find a Grave's own structured data for Barnabas Cantwell (1813 Muskingum County, Ohio - 1883, buried Blue Valley Cemetery, Ayr, Adams County, Nebraska) independently confirms parents Thomas William Cantwell (1784-1830, Coshocton County, Ohio) and Jemima Kelley Cantwell Baker (1790-1837) -- corroborating the forum's Thomas/Jemima claim -- three wives (Mary J. Lane m.1836 Muskingum Co.; Elizabeth Ellen Taylor m.1851 Henry Co. Iowa, d.1866 Henry Co., most likely Sarah Alice's actual mother; and a third wife named either \"Mary A. Ogg\" or \"Mary Ann Anderson Hyde\" depending on the source, m.1868), and the family's relocation from Henry County, Iowa to Adams County, Nebraska between the 1870 and 1880 censuses -- which lines up exactly with Sarah Alice's own obituary detail about Hastings, Nebraska. Critically, Sarah Alice Cantwell does NOT appear on the structured 'Children' lists of either Barnabas or Elizabeth Taylor on Find a Grave; the only link is a hedged 2012 visitor bio note saying she was \"possibly\" a daughter. A FamilyTreeDNA Cantwell surname-project page independently lists a Y-DNA test-taker whose earliest known ancestor 'might be' this same Barnabas Barney Cantwell, and separately preserves a Dublin-Ireland-to-Coshocton-County-Ohio oral-history origin story (immigrant surname said to have been 'Kentwell' before becoming 'Cantwell,' via an elopement with Margaret O'Brien around 1772) -- echoing but not identical to the forum's William/Margaret O'Brien claim, and not confirmed to be the same DNA-family branch. The jurisdiction stack for future record pulls is now much more specific: Wayne Township/Mt. Pleasant, Henry County, Iowa -> Ayr/Hastings, Adams County, Nebraska -> Glencoe, Payne County, Oklahoma, with Muskingum and Coshocton Counties, Ohio one generation further back, and an unconfirmed Dublin, Ireland origin beyond that.

### Wave 2

Rebuilt the Cantwell line frame by recovering nearly the full genealogy.com forum thread (9 of 10 messages: 760, 761, 763, 858, 859, 861, 868, 909, 920, 1073 -- all Cloudflare-gated but penetrable with a browser UA and 15-20s retry backoffs) and cross-checking it against a dozen Find a Grave memorials. The forum thread yields a full 1860/1870 Henry County, Iowa census-based sibling roster for Sarah Alice (Hesikiah, Alonzo, Luther from an earlier Ohio marriage; Margaret, Thomas, Juliette, Martha, George Franklin/Frank, Sarah Alice, and Elizabeth from the Elizabeth Taylor years; Oscar and Dilla/Della from the third-wife years) and a direct researcher statement that Elizabeth Taylor -- not the third wife -- was Sarah Alice's mother, based on the 1864 birth predating the 1868 remarriage. Find a Grave's structured data independently corroborates most of this (Elizabeth Ellen Taylor Cantwell 1828-1866, her father Samuel Taylor 1804-1893, her 7 structured children none of whom include Sarah Alice, and Barnabas's parents Thomas William Cantwell 1784-1830/Jemima Kelley 1790-1837) but also exposes a three-way naming conflict for Barnabas's third wife -- 'Mary A. Ogg' (forum + a 2012 Find a Grave visitor note), 'Mary Ann Anderson Hyde' (Find a Grave's own spouse field on both Barnabas's and son Oscar's pages), and 'Nancy Ellen Farmer Cantwell Wilder' (Find a Grave's parent field on daughter Elizabeth Jane Ruby's page) -- none of which can be resolved without pulling the actual 26 Jul 1868 Henry County, Iowa marriage record. The Irish-origin story was independently verified via FamilyTreeDNA's Cantwell project page (Margaret O'Brien eloping with 'William Kentwell' c.1772, settling in Coshocton County, Ohio) but its exact phrasing -- 'his surname was William but family research indicates he was known as Thomas' -- raises a new structural question of whether this immigrant IS Thomas William Cantwell himself rather than his father, and no Ohio county history, probate, or land record was found to test it. On the Mundell side, Find a Grave's children list for John Pryor Mundell now names four additional children with full married surnames and life dates (Nellie May Post 1889-1946, Maude Tucker 1895-1986, Thomas Samuel Mundell 1897-1956, Velma E Cantner 1902-1990), very likely accounting for 4 of the '5 survivors' in Sarah Alice's 1939 obituary, and forum message 861 surfaces a wholly new, unverified claim that John and Sarah Alice divorced by the 1930 census, with John remaining in Rose Township, Payne County, Oklahoma and Sarah Alice moving into Glencoe.

### Wave 3

This wave resolves the predecessor's central Cantwell puzzle and pushes the line one further generation back with new open, non-forum sources. (1) An independently discovered 1888 Henry County, Iowa county-history biography of Samuel Cantwell (Barnabas's brother), hosted free at iagenweb.org, corroborates the whole Thomas Cantwell/Jemima Kelley parentage, the Coshocton County Ohio origin, the '10 children, 3 survivors' detail, Jemima's remarriage to John Baker, and Barnabas's own Ohio->Iowa (1846-1874)->Adams Co Nebraska (d.1883) migration -- all independently of Find a Grave and the genealogy.com forum. (2) Direct Find a Grave pulls (curl + browser UA, no retries needed this session) resolved the predecessor's three-way 'third wife' naming conflict: 'Nancy Ellen Farmer Cantwell Wilder' is NOT Barnabas's wife at all -- her own 1918 obituary, transcribed on her Find a Grave memorial, shows she married Barnabas's SON Hezekiah Cantwell (1838-1886, himself a child of Barnabas's first wife Mary J. Lane, m.1836) in 1863, and her nine children with Hezekiah include exactly the 'Mary Ann Cantwell Shuler,' 'Charles Hezekiah Cantwell,' and 'Elizabeth Jane Cantwell Ruby' that the predecessor flagged as an unexplained sibling cluster. That whole cluster is one generation down from Barnabas -- his grandchildren, not his children -- which also fully explains why their 1864-1869 birth years didn't fit an 1868 marriage. The real third-wife conflict narrows to two candidates for the same woman: 'Mary A. Ogg' (2012 Find a Grave visitor bio on Barnabas's page) and 'Mary Ann Anderson Hyde' (Find a Grave's own structured spouse field, b.1842 Milford Center OH, d.1916 Kansas City MO, mother of Oscar and Dilla Cantwell) -- these may be the same person under two different married surnames (Hyde predates Barnabas; Ogg could postdate his 1883 death), but no marriage or death record has been found to confirm it. (3) The 1885/1886 marriage-date discrepancy and the forum's 1930-divorce claim are both resolved by Sarah Alice's own 1939 Payne County News obituary (quoted on her Find a Grave page): it explicitly gives 1885 for the marriage and states John P. Mundell 'preceded her in death April 10, 1938,' directly contradicting the forum's divorce claim. (4) On the immigrant generation: Find a Grave's own structured data (Thomas William Cantwell m. Jemima Kelley in 1804, Thomas b.1784) makes it far more consistent for the 1772 Ireland-elopement couple (William 'Kentwell'/Margaret O'Brien, per FamilyTreeDNA's Cantwell project) to be Thomas's PARENTS rather than Thomas himself, since an adult 1772 emigre could not have been born in 1784. However, both Thomas's and Jemima's Find a Grave birthplaces ('Coshocton County, Ohio' in 1784/1790) are almost certainly anachronistic back-projections, since Coshocton County was not organized until 1811 and the area was not open to Euro-American settlement until after the 1795 Treaty of Greenville -- meaning the family's actual pre-Ohio path (likely through Pennsylvania, Maryland, or Virginia) remains completely undocumented. A separate 2000-era genealogy.com thread ('origin of cantwell family,' msgs 432-454, not previously pulled) gives the general Cantwell/de Conteville/Kentwell surname history and places the family's historic Irish heartland in Kilkenny/Tipperary/Waterford (confirmed independently on Wikipedia), not Dublin -- a real tension with the oral-history 'Dublin' claim that has not been reconciled, and that thread's other replies show a distinct, unrelated colonial-American Cantwell line (Capt. Edmund Cantwell of Delaware) that must not be conflated with this family. No primary Coshocton County probate, land, or pre-1811 Muskingum County marriage record has yet been found for Thomas Cantwell or his parents, and the Henry County, Iowa 1868 marriage register itself (which would settle Ogg vs. Hyde definitively) was not locatable in any open index. Nebraska Gravestones' Blue Valley (Ayr) Cemetery search/query endpoints are confirmed this session to sit behind a genuine Cloudflare JS managed challenge (not a simple rate-limit) -- curl retries cannot get past it; only the static cemetery landing page loads.
