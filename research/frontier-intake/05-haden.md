Starting from the already-documented working lead (Elizabeth J. Haden Dible -> parents Andrew Jackson Haden + Sophia Reid/Reed -> grandparents Richard D. VA 1799 marriage), I pushed the Haden line two further colonial-Virginia generations back using a heavily source-cited compiled genealogy (sites.rootsweb.com/~katy/haden, apparently by the same "Angela Kay Mayer" who compiles the already-cited KaysAncestry RootsMagic pages) cross-checked against an independently authored genealogy blog (moreleaves.blogspot.com) and three Find a Grave memorials fetched directly. Jean/Jenny Moseley) -> Joseph Haden (1752-1820, Fluvanna Co. VA militia captain and VA Ratifying Convention delegate, m. Mary Peatross of Caroline Co.) -> Richard D. Haden (1776-1837?, m. The single biggest problem surfaced is a direct conflict in Richard D. All claims here remain at "compiled" confidence pending pulls of the actual Virginia Chronicle newspaper image, the LVA Chancery Records Index for Fluvanna County, the Campbell Co. Haden (1 white male, 2 slaves, 1 horse) and his father Joseph Haden (2 white males, 8 slaves, 5 horses, 1 carriage), which matches and details the "1802 personal property tax already noted" reference in the task brief. A speculative, unconfirmed lead ties Sally Tompkins to the prominent William Tompkins/Elizabeth Cobbs family of "Whitehall," Albemarle Co., but no source connects that family to a Haden marriage, so it is flagged as speculative only, not a finding."

### Wave 2

Building on the predecessor's Anthony Haden -> John Haden -> Joseph Haden -> Richard D. Haden -> Andrew Jackson Haden chain, this wave made two main moves. First, it sharpened (without fully resolving) the central open question: a Fluvanna Co. chancery notice transcribed on the compiler's site explicitly lists Richard D. Haden as '[deceased]' by 1 Mar 1821 and names all eight of his children (including 'Andrew J.'), while a separate family document shows him alive in Virginia in 1820 signing a marriage permission -- pinning a likely death window of late 1820/early 1821 in Virginia. Countering this, a previously-unlocated Find a Grave memorial for Richard D. Haden does exist (correcting the predecessor's claim that none did) supporting the '15 Feb 1837, Cumberland Co. KY' tradition, but it has no photo, inscription, or cited source, though it is maintained by an independent, credentialed local Cumberland Co. KY genealogist rather than by the Virginia-side compiler -- leaving real tension rather than resolution. Second, and more consequential for research strategy: this wave discovered that nearly all of the 'colonial Virginia push-back' evidence the predecessor treated as cross-corroborated across independent sources (moreleaves.blogspot.com, sites.rootsweb.com/~katy, sites.rootsmagic.com/KaysAncestry, and a newly-found PDF at homepages.rootsweb.com/~lksstarr) is authored by one single compiler, Kay Haden -- confirmed by matching author attribution/contact info across all four. That same compiler's own 2013 methodological paper is valuable in that it debunks fabricated 'immigrant Anthony Haden' traditions and instead proposes, as explicitly unproven, a King William Co. VA 'John Hayden' (real 1701 land patent, real 1704 quit rent entry) as Anthony's possible father -- a genuine but speculative push into 1670s-1700s colonial Virginia. Independently, a Find a Grave bio for John Haden (1723-1817) pushed the maternal Moseley/Nichols line back to the 1680s-1720s in Middlesex Co. VA. Frames below give jurisdiction stacks and concrete free record sets (LVA Chancery Records Index, Virginia Chronicle, LVA Land Office Patents, Christ Church Parish register, FamilySearch KY marriages) for the next wave to pull primary images, since virtually nothing in this line has yet been verified beyond compiled transcriptions or index-level Find a Grave entries."

### Wave 3

This wave pushed past the predecessor's compiled Kay-Haden-authored narrative into a mix of genuine primary and independent government-catalog sources. The strongest new result is direct, verbatim confirmation from the freely digitized Christ Church Parish Register (Middlesex Co., VA) of the Moseley/Nichols marriage (10 Oct 1717), their daughter Jean/Jenny's baptism (14 Oct 1722), and Mary Nichols Moseley's death (17 Jan 1730) -- upgrading that whole maternal link from compiled Find A Grave text to true verified-primary status. Second, a raw curl of moreleaves.blogspot.com (Kay Haden's own writing) added a previously unreported 1699 land record for 'John Hayden' (370 acres via George Adams, traceable to a 1679 Pamunkey Indian lease) and an exact Patent Book 9, p.368 citation for the 1701 patent -- concrete targets for pulling the actual state-held (fire-immune) images. Third, pulling Kay Haden's own signed PDF directly clarified an important nuance: her Y-DNA statement excludes the Massachusetts/Maryland/Spotsylvania Hayden lines from relation to Anthony Haden, but does NOT test the King William 'John Hayden' father-candidate at all -- and a direct check of the FamilyTreeDNA Hayden project's own pages confirmed it says nothing about this specific candidate, so that hypothesis remains genuinely untested, not merely 'unproven.' Fourth, the Library of Virginia's own (non-Haden-authored) records catalog directly confirms King William County's court records were destroyed by an 1885 fire, and an independent burned-counties reference confirms King & Queen and Hanover counties are similarly gutted -- this is the concrete, permanent archival reason the trail cannot currently be pushed past Anthony Haden (b.1694) via Virginia county records. Fifth, successfully curling a previously-inaccessible, richly detailed Find A Grave memorial for Joseph Haden turned up Richard D. Haden's full name (Richard Douglas Haden), his complete 8-child list (cross-confirming the predecessor's 1821 chancery-notice report independently), new facts (Andrew Jackson Haden died 1865 in Cumberland Co. KY; Sophia Reed died Nov 1872), and an exact LVA chancery case number (065-1822-008, 'John N Haden v. Legatees of Joseph Haden'), which was then independently verified to exist in LVA's own Chancery Records Index. Its digital images are loadable but JS-gated and could not be read this wave -- pulling them in a real browser is now the single highest-value next step for the Richard D. Haden VA-vs-KY death question. One caution for future waves: an earlier aggregated web-search answer claimed the Cumberland Co. KY 'Haden Cemetery' held 471 memorials; a direct fetch of the actual page disproved this (it genuinely has only 3, confirming the predecessor's original figure) -- a reminder that AI-summarized search results must be checked against the raw page before being reported as fact. Bottom line for the FINAL PUSH: the documentary trail for this Haden/Hayden line hits a hard, explainable wall at Anthony Haden (b. 26 Aug 1694, per an unverifiable Bible note; d. 1763/1774, Hanover Co. VA), who was demonstrably Virginia-raised (not an immigrant) and whose only candidate father -- 'John Hayden' of King William/King & Queen Co. (1699-1711 land records) -- cannot currently be proven or refuted because the county court records that would settle it were destroyed in 1885 (King William), and King & Queen and Hanover are likewise burned-record counties; the only paths that remain open are (1) pulling the surviving state-level Land Office patent image itself (Patent Book 9, p.368), (2) locating a free copy of English Duplicates of Lost Virginia Records for fuller 1699 context, and (3) identifying a documented living male-line descendant of the King William 'John Hayden' for Y-DNA comparison against Anthony Haden's already-tested line -- absent any of those, no English emigration origin or earlier colonial generation can currently be established for this family.
