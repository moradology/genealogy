## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Julius Henry Zodrow (b. Jun 1869 Wisconsin, d. 13 Mar 1959 Medical Lake, Spokane Co., WA; buried Holy Cross Cemetery, Spokane) sits inside a well-documented three-stage family migration: Kansas (Sheridan/Decatur Co.) -> the Camas Prairie German-Catholic colony around Cottonwood/Ferdinand, Idaho County, Idaho (settled from 1895) -> Spokane, Washington. Julius's own traceable children (August Henry, Leonard William, Henry G, Rose E, Theodore Paul, Gertrude A, Ida A) overwhelmingly died in the Spokane/Stevens Co./Vancouver WA area, several buried at Holy Cross Cemetery alongside him, and daughter Ida A. was born in Cottonwood, Idaho Co., ID in 1914, pinning the KS->ID leg to before that year. Two of Julius's siblings (Albert Zodrow, Hedwig "Hattie" Zodrow Schroer) independently relocated to the same Ferdinand, Idaho Co. ID community and stayed there for life, while five other siblings (Leonard Ferdinand, Frank C, John J, Martha Fleckenstein, Anthony Michael) never left the Sheridan/Decatur Co. KS home area - so the Washington move was specific to Julius's own household, not the whole sibling generation. A major, previously unflagged complication: Find a Grave's own reciprocal family links show Cecilia Antoinette Zodrow Zimmerman's PARENTS as Leonard Ferdinand Zodrow + Catherine Fleckenstein Zodrow (both lifelong Kansas residents, matching Cecilia's own lifelong-Kansas record), NOT Julius Henry Zodrow + Ernestina Baier as the current working artifact assumes. If that linkage is correct, Julius is Cecilia's uncle, not her father, and "Cecilia's siblings' Kansas-to-Washington migration" is actually her first cousins' migration - this needs a primary-record check (1910/1920 census, KS death certificate, or parish baptismal register) before the Zodrow/Baier line in the artifact is trusted as written. On the 1868 immigration question, no primary Hamburg passenger-list image or index entry was directly viewed in this session; the specific claim that John and Antonette (Grams) Zodrow sailed Hamburg-to-New York on the SS Allemannia in Aug 1868 out of Lebehnke traces to a compiled Geni.com profile and a Cloudflare-gated genealogy.com family page, neither fetchable as raw text in this session, so it remains compiled/unverified pending a primary-index pull. Concrete free pull paths exist: FamilySearch's Klüber Index 1 (1856-1871) card range "Wittach-Zywicki+unknowns" (FHL item 201345, items 1-3) for the actual Hamburg departure entry, and FamilySearch's free "Germans to America Index, 1850-1897" (collection 2110801), which indexes the print volume (Vol. 21, May-Sep 1868) covering the claimed arrival window.

### Wave 2

This wave overturns the predecessor's central open question rather than merely confirming it: Find a Grave's own reciprocal family-tree links, checked directly on both sides, prove that Cecilia Antoinette Zodrow Zimmerman (Doyle Zimmerman's mother) is the daughter of Julius Henry Zodrow's brother Leonard Ferdinand Zodrow and Catherine Fleckenstein Zodrow - not of Julius and Ernestina Baier as index.html's 'Zodrow/Baier Line' currently states. Julius's own Find a Grave page lists a full Children set (August Henry, Leonard William, Henry G, Rose E, Theodore Paul, Gertrude A, Ida A) that excludes Cecilia, while Leonard Ferdinand's page reciprocally lists Cecilia among his children alongside Leo Julius, Mary Margaret, and Arnold Carl - all three of whom, checked individually, died in Kansas and never migrated. This means the task's premise of 'Cecilia's siblings' migration to the Pacific Northwest' does not exist for her real siblings; the Spokane/Idaho movement belongs solely to Julius's own children, her first cousins. A second major correction surfaced independently from two sources (Washington State Archives' own Holy Cross Cemetery burial register and Find a Grave): Julius's wife Ernestina/Ernestine Zodrow was buried 11 May 1940, not 1963 as currently recorded, in the family plot immediately next to Julius's own 1959 grave - making Julius a 19-year widower, a detail that sharpens (without yet proving) the 'why an elderly widower ended up in a Medical Lake institution' theory. New primary-source land-record work via BLM's free GLO database traced the immigrant Zodrow household's actual US land trail: a 40-acre 1876 Homestead Act patent in Marquette County, Wisconsin (Wausau land office); an 1885 Republic County, Kansas patent; and matching 1900 Sheridan County, Kansas patents for both John Zodrow and, separately, for Julius's wife Ernstina Baier Zodrow (165.63 acres, Colby land office) - while an exhaustive nationwide-plus-Idaho-specific BLM search under every plausible Zodrow spelling returned zero Idaho patents, meaning Julius's Idaho land (if any) was privately acquired, not homesteaded. The Washington State Archives' Holy Cross Cemetery collection also surfaced three previously unidentified daughters-in-law (Ella Mae, Frances Hannah, and Gertrude Mary Zodrow) and confirmed Albert L. Zodrow's burial location, while newly showing Frances Hannah Zodrow (Leonard William's wife) was Pennsylvania-born and died in Chewelah, Stevens County WA in 1952 - identifying a second family cluster beyond Spokane proper. On the 1868 Hamburg question, this session confirmed concretely that the Hamburg Passenger Lists are Ancestry-exclusive for images/full index (paywalled) and that FamilySearch's free partial index is blocked from non-interactive access by bot-protection, i.e., a genuine login wall; a free, no-login alternative (Castle Garden/Liberty Ellis Foundation NYC arrivals, which predate Ellis Island) is identified but not yet pulled.

### Wave 3

This wave's central contribution is finally locating the family's Idaho stopover on the ground: free records (a 1961 Lewiston Tribune obituary for Julius's sister Hedwig Zodrow Schroer, a Lewiston Morning Tribune obituary for nephew George Louis Zodrow, Albert Zodrow's Find a Grave page, and the free BYU-Idaho Idaho State Death Index) together document that Julius's brother Albert Zodrow and sister Hedwig both left Kansas for the Camas Prairie/Ferdinand/Cottonwood area of Idaho County, Idaho around 1910-1911, that Albert died at Lewiston (Nez Perce County) in 1948, and that this Idaho branch (his wife, two grandchildren, and son Fred) persisted there into the 1940s before some descendants (Fred's widow, nephew George) later relocated to Spokane -- explaining, at the extended-family level, the 'Idaho then Washington' pattern the task asked about, even though no record yet places Julius's own nuclear household in Idaho itself. Separately, Julius's actual 1959 Washington death certificate was obtained for free from the Washington State Digital Archives and confirms Eastern State Hospital as his death location, gives a conflicting birth year (1870, not 1869), and documents a Nov 1956 admission; parallel free certificates for sons Leonard William (Kansas-born, Spokane-area mining crusher operator, died with chronic schizophrenia noted) and August Henry (Kansas-born, WWII-era Vancouver aluminum-plant veteran) supply concrete economic reasons two branches ended up in Washington, and surface a previously undocumented son, Gordon Zodrow. The prior wave's corrected genealogy -- Cecilia Antoinette Zodrow Zimmerman as daughter of Leonard Ferdinand Zodrow and Catherine Fleckenstein, not of Julius -- was independently re-verified by fetching the Find a Grave pages directly rather than trusting the index characterization. The 1868 Hamburg SS Allemannia claim (home village Lebehnke) remains unverified on any free record after checking five separate 1868 Allemannia voyage transcriptions; it still traces only to a compiled citation of an Ancestry-paywalled Hamburg departure image, and the deepest open thread -- John Zodrow's and Antonette Grams's own Prussian parents -- remains genuinely unresolved pending microfilm-level Lebehnke parish records.
