## Where this sits in the tree

Donna Connelly's paternal side carries three open conflicts: James Connelly's unknown Kentucky parents (case.14); David Monroe Durham 1832-1897 vs. a compiled 1829-1887 doppelganger -- one man or two (case.15; his birth/marriage/death are strong events on the Connelly plate); and the Ford/Staples layer -- John Laborn Ford's father (John Laborn Sr. vs. Levi, plus the Apr 1840 vs. 30 Mar 1842 marriage) is case.16, anchored by the 1911 family letter and the Caswell County, NC records. John Burton Staples and Nancy Ann Turner birth/migration events already sit on the plate as leads.

## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Building on the already-documented Connelly/Durham/Ford/Staples chain in index.html (James Connelly m. America Ellen Durham; David Monroe Durham 1832 NC-1897 Nebo KY m. Virginia Ann Ford 1857 Menard Co. IL; Virginia Ann Ford's parents John Laborn Ford and Lucy Ann Staples per an 1911 son-authored letter; the unresolved Caswell Co. NC 'John Laborn Ford Sr. vs. Levi Ford' parentage conflict), this pass pulled full original text for three of the four cited Muhlenberg County Heritage newsletter issues via Wayback Machine (WKU's own site blocks automated fetch), plus a live Find A Grave pull for James Connelly and the Caswell County Historical Association blog post in full. Two findings reframe open questions: (1) James Connelly's own Find A Grave memorial gives his birthplace as New York, not Kentucky -- a detail not mentioned in the existing source-index blurb and worth verifying before assuming a Kentucky-native origin; (2) a 1983-published Durham family Bible transcription gives the full 8-child Durham sibling list with exact dates and shows three daughters married into the same Hibbs family, plus Virginia Ann Ford Durham's precise death date (16 May 1931). The Ford parentage conflict got sharper: two independent 1980s newsletter queries put John Laborn Ford's death in 1898 at Muhlenberg Co./Greenville, while Otto Rothert's 1913 published county history says 1897 near Friendship -- a genuine, checkable one-year conflict. The Caswell County Historical Association post (read in full) adds that the 'Levi Ford' theory is explicitly undocumented beyond Ancestry.com trees and names a previously unflagged claimed sibling, Simeon Preston Ford. A parallel Caswell-to-Muhlenberg Ford migration (Eli Ford of Hogans Creek) gives a useful locality/timeframe control case. Jurisdiction-level facts were pulled fresh from FamilySearch's wiki and Wikipedia: Hopkins Co. KY suffered courthouse record losses in 1829 and 1864, Muhlenberg Co. KY has no known record loss and marriages survive from 1802, and Caswell Co. NC was formed 1 June 1777 from Orange County. Several genuinely relevant free sources (WikiTree profiles, the Caswell County compiled genealogy site, the Illinois Statewide Marriage Index, FamilySearch ancestors.org tree pages) could not be read in this pass due to bot-detection walls or JS shells and are flagged as needing a manual browser pass rather than as confirmed empty.

### Wave 2

This pass verified the predecessor's two flagged conflicts against a primary-adjacent published source (Otto Rothert's 1913 "A History of Muhlenberg County," full text pulled from Internet Archive) and, more importantly, against an actual transcribed county marriage register and marriage-bond index -- and it overturned one of the predecessor's leading hypotheses rather than confirming it. Rothert's book (which quotes a letter Rev. G. W. Ford wrote directly to the author) sides with the 1911 family letter on John Laborn Ford's 1897 death near Friendship, weakening the two 1980s newsletter queries' "1898/Greenville" claim -- but Rothert's book also repeats the family's "1840" marriage date, and checking that date against the actual Muhlenberg County, Kentucky marriage register (Book 2, page 20, KYGenWeb transcription) shows the real civil marriage date was 20 March 1842, not 1840 -- meaning both "family tradition" sources trace to one shared voice (G.W. Ford's own memory), not independent corroboration, and the primary record overrules them. On the Caswell County "Levi Ford" parentage theory, this pass traced the claim to its actual documentary root (a genealogy.com compiled register for Simeon Ford of Hogans Creek, NC, citing his 1833-34 estate partition) and then checked the underlying Caswell County marriage-bond index directly: it shows no 1806 Levi Ford x Aritta Davis marriage at all, only a 1814 Levi Ford x Mary Payne marriage, and it shows the "Laban Ford" from the same estate partition died in Greene County, Missouri -- demonstrably not "our" John Laborn Ford, despite the near-identical name. A newly found Muhlenberg marriage-register entry ("K.S. Ford, born Caswell Co., NC," 1853) documents a third, previously unflagged Caswell-to-Muhlenberg Ford migrant worth investigating as a relative. On the Connelly side, James Connelly's Find a Grave birthplace field ("New York, USA") was directly re-verified, and a check of every other Connelly burial in his own cemetery (Union Cemetery, Nebo) turned up only his descendants and no elder generation -- a small negative-evidence point consistent with an out-of-Kentucky origin, though his actual parents remain completely unidentified. David Monroe Durham's North Carolina county of origin and the Staples/Turner Virginia-Tennessee counties remain open; Rothert's book, read in full, adds nothing on either. The Illinois Statewide Marriage Index and most Muhlenberg County Heritage back issues remain blocked by bot walls or WKU's non-cooperative server and are flagged as needing a manual, logged-in pass rather than confirmed empty.

### Wave 3

This pass re-verified the predecessor's two resolved conflicts and then went substantially further, using direct fetches (not search-summary paraphrase) of Find A Grave, Otto Rothert's 1913 full text, the actual Muhlenberg County KY marriage register transcription, a genealogy.com forum thread, and the Caswell County Historical Association's own blog. The single biggest development: the "David Monroe Durham 1829-1887 vs. 1832-1897" conflict index.html currently treats as a minor "index-snippet" issue is actually a full, internally-consistent, cross-linked identity split on Find A Grave itself -- a memorial giving 1829-1887/Virginia-born/buried-at-Durham-Cemetery-Madisonville is directly linked as spouse of Virginia Ann Ford Durham and father of America Ellen Durham Connelly, i.e. it is THE primary linked-parent record for this line, not a stray child-page mention. Separately, John Laborn Ford's own Find A Grave memorial gives a third specific death date (12 Apr 1898), reopening rather than closing the 1897/1898 question the predecessor believed Rothert had settled. On the positive side, this pass fully closed the predecessor's open question about where the debunked "Levi Ford x Aritta Davis, 1806, Caswell Co." theory actually originates: it traces to two independent but equally uncited sources -- a private correspondent's descendancy chart named on genealogy.com, and Ancestry.com public trees per the CCHA blog's own admission -- neither a documentary record. Kerney S. Ford, the predecessor's unidentified "K.S. Ford," was identified by full name via a later witness signature in the same marriage register, confirming him as a long-term Muhlenberg resident and a second real Caswell-to-Muhlenberg Ford migrant. James Connelly's Kentucky death certificate -- still the single highest-value unpulled record -- was confirmed genuinely absent from the one free Hopkins County transcription that should list it, meaning it truly requires a login-gated pull. David Monroe Durham's NC/VA county of origin and the Staples/Turner Virginia counties remain fully open; a promising WikiTree lead for a Staples ancestor could not be verified because wikitree.com is now behind a JavaScript bot-challenge that survived repeated user-agent rotation. No documented immigrant-generation or European-parish record exists anywhere in this line -- the hard wall sits at John Burton Staples (b.1785 VA) and Nancy Ann Turner (b.1782 VA), and at John Laborn Ford (b.1811 NC) with unverified parents; the "Scotland 1767" story is unsupported anywhere outside a single 1911 letter, confirmed this pass by a full-text search of Rothert's independent 1913 account.
