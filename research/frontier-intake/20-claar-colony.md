## Where this sits in the tree

Donna's mother Martha Frances Claar descends via Samuel Dove Claar (the Hoyle-vs-Hogle 1895 Oberlin marriage record is case.17) and Henry Claar (documented birth/death events on the plate) back to the Bedford County, PA Claar colony (Claysburg/Blue Knob), through Samuel Claar + Lydia Stropes. The 1813 Bedford Orphan's Court record ('Samuel Claar son of Simon Claar', primary) and the 1991 biography debunking the died-at-sea legend came from wave 1; waves 2-3 reached Balthasar Clar of Canton Bern via Mimbach, Palatinate, with wave 3 fixing the honest documentary end at Balthasar Hans Clar (b. 1647, d. 1703 Mimbach -- his own parents unproven).

## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Starting from the already-documented spine (Henry Claar 1838-1915 x Electa Alice White 1838-1904; Samuel Claar x Lydia Stropes; George Washington Claar x Barbara House; Daniel White x Sarah Osborn), I pulled a 1991 self-published Claar biography with reproduced Bedford County PA primary records, cross-checked WikiTree profiles that cite actual FamilySearch census/divorce ARK links, and pulled Find a Grave memorials for the Ohio and Kansas branches. Three findings reshape the frame: (1) both the Claar and Stropes families independently converge on Franklin Township, Jackson County, Ohio by 1830, which is the real locus for Henry Claar's 1838 birth and the 1859 Claar-White marriage; (2) Daniel White and Sarah Osborn's own residence traces to Harrison Township (later Minford), Scioto County, Ohio, not Jackson County - an adjoining-county correction; (3) there is a live name collision in Bedford Borough PA circa 1799-1800 between two unrelated "Samuel Claar"s (one the immigrant Johan Simon Clar's own late-life son, one our ancestor George Washington Claar Sr.'s son), which any Bedford County record search must disambiguate. I could not confirm that the Stropes family has any Bedford County PA origin at all - the only sourced WikiTree profile for John/Johannes Stropes flags his birthplace as either Wysox, Bradford County PA (a different, far-northeastern PA county) or Catskill, Greene County NY, both explicitly marked "uncertain." The German-colony Lutheran/Reformed church-book trail for the Claar immigrant line (Johan Simon Clar, baptized Mimbach, Palatinate, Germany) runs through St. Matthew's Lutheran Church, Hanover, York County PA, not Bedford County proper, and the Bedford Borough Lutheran/Reformed records themselves are mostly unpublished and held only at the Pioneer Historical Society of Bedford County, not free online. The "Claar Congregation" Church of the Brethren in the Claysburg/Blue Knob area (Upper/Lower Claar, Kimmel Twp Bedford Co/Greenfield Twp Blair Co) was founded by Frederick Claar and Christina Walter - Frederick was Samuel Claar Sr.'s full brother, but that Brethren congregation is a collateral-branch institution, not proven to be our direct line's church.

### Wave 2

Verified and pushed the Claar colony frontier one full generation deeper (Balthasar Clar of Canton Bern -> Mimbach, Palatinate -> Jacob Clar & Anna Marie Reber, both dying in 1740 during emigration) via Frontier Patriots' biographical sketch and cross-checking WikiTree's Claar-51/Claar-39 ancestor panels, which independently agree on the 1740 double-death date and thereby correct the predecessor's flagged 'died at sea circa 1765' oral tradition with a specific, checkable 16 May 1747 Sauer-newspaper-ad story instead. Clarified that our direct line descends from Johan Simon Clar's FIRST wife Anna Margaretha Klee (Hanover, York Co PA), while Simon's own move to Bedford Borough came only in 1795 with a second wife - meaning Frederick Claar (the Claar-Walter Brethren founder) is Samuel Claar Sr.'s uncle, not brother as the predecessor stated. Found and directly fetched a previously-missed free transcription of Bedford Borough's Trinity Lutheran/St. John's Reformed Union Church register (mowerfamily.org), which documents a second, Bedford-resident 'Samuel Claar' (wife Rahel) baptizing a child in Oct 1827 - concrete, dated evidence sharpening the predecessor's 'two Samuel Claars' collision into an actual record conflict - plus an unplaced 'John Claar' household (wife Mary Ann, d.1846). Corroborated via Find a Grave that Lydia Stropes died in 1870 (not the WikiTree ancestor-panel's erroneous, contamination-suspect '1904'), that Daniel White died in 1857 (not 1851 as implied by the existing FamilySearch citation), and that Mary Myrtle Claar is a genuine, census-documented daughter of Henry Claar and Electa White. Surfaced a hard three-way naming conflict on Daniel White's mother (Rebecca Solace vs. an unverifiable 'Rebecca Sollis' vs. WikiTree's 'Sarah Comfort') and flagged that an AI web-search summary fabricated/misattributed the 'Sollis' detail - it does not appear anywhere in the actual Find a Grave page. Reinforced (with a directly-fetched Bradsby's-history quote) that the only substantive PA paper trail for the Stropes surname points to an unrelated Bradford County/Greene County NY Dutch 'Stroop' family, strengthening the case that the frontier topic's 'unified German colony' framing may not apply to the Stropes side at all.

### Wave 3

Pushed the direct Clar/Claar patrilineal line to its honest documentary end (Balthasar Hans Clar, b.1647 Canton Bern, d.1703 Mimbach - his own parents are explicitly unresearched on WikiTree) and added one full new generation on the collateral Hussong side (Balthasar's wife's parents, Antonius Hussong & Catherine, both already resident in Mimbach by the 1630s-1699, meaning the Hussongs, not the Clars, were the established Palatinate family). Directly fetched WikiTree's Johann Simon Clar profile (Clar-6) and confirmed it names Samuel Claar - the son of Simon's SECOND wife Eva Catherine Lingenfelter - as living with his mother in Bedford Borough until her 1840 death, giving a concrete (though still unproven) identity for the predecessor's 'second Samuel Claar + wife Rahel' from the 1827 Bedford church register. Separately fetched (via Wayback, since WikiTree's live site is now behind an AWS WAF bot-challenge) the compiled profile for OUR Samuel Claar Sr. (Claar-51) and found WikiTree's own data-quality system has flagged it 'CONFLATED WITH CLAAR-47' - a structural warning that the whole compiled lineage this frontier has been built on may mix two different men; that same profile also states the 1827 Samuel-Claar/Lydia-Stropes marriage happened 'in Ohio,' not Bedford County PA, which is independent circumstantial support for two distinct Samuel Claars rather than proof. Pulled six free full-text digitizations of Blackburn's 1906 'History of Bedford and Somerset Counties, Pennsylvania' from archive.org (not previously used) and extracted real Bedford Borough tax rolls (1796, c.1800, 1810s, 1860) naming a cluster of Claars - Simon, John, Henry (saddler), George's heirs, Rachel, Sophia, John Jr. - that, cross-referenced against WikiTree's list of Simon Clar's 12 children, plausibly identifies the 'unplaced John Claar' as Simon's own son Johan Claar (b.1769) and the tax-roll 'Henry Claar, saddler' as his son Henry (b.1777) - both full brothers of our George Washington Claar Sr. who stayed in Bedford Borough instead of going to Ohio. This is an unconfirmed inference, not a sourced fact. Searched exhaustively for a Stropes/Stroup Bedford County PA paper trail across the Bedford County Historical Society index and all six Blackburn digitizations: zero hits for Stropes/Strope/Stroup in any of them, reinforcing the predecessor's suspicion that the Bedford PA 'colony' framing may not apply to the Stropes side. A search-engine synthesis (not a direct page fetch - WikiTree blocked it) surfaced a possible 'John Stropes Jr., b.abt 1804 PA, Franklin Township, Jackson Co OH, m. Leah Mercer' who would be a plausible brother of Lydia living in the identical Ohio township as the Claars; this is explicitly NOT verified against the actual page and must not be treated as fact. The same discipline applies to a search-engine-only hit for 'Abel White (bef.1760-1844) m. Hannah Closson, Rockingham VT' - a tempting match for the Vermont-birth tradition but unconfirmed by direct fetch (403 error) and its wife's name does not match any of the three candidates already in conflict for Daniel White's mother, so it must be checked, not assumed. Also surfaced (secondary/collateral) a full additional maternal ancestor panel for Barbara House (GW Claar Sr.'s wife) reaching a second, independent Baden-Palatinate immigrant, Johann Valentine Haus Sr., plus MD/VA colonial Eagon-Wood ancestors - outside the strict Claar/Klar patriline but part of 'as far back as records allow.'
