## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Working from index.html's existing 'working lead' status for John F. Rust + Ethel B. Rupert as Mary Frances Rust's candidate parents, I fetched the actual Find A Grave memorial pages (not just search snippets) for Mary Frances Rust Strawn and her family cluster, using a browser-UA curl since raw WebFetch hit browser-compatibility and 403 walls on FamilySearch and the Genealogy.com forum. The result is a materially stronger, mutually cross-linked Find A Grave family group: Mary Frances Rust Strawn's own memorial (115684712) independently lists both parents -- F John Rust (1870-1909) and Etta B. Rupert Rust Wells (1876-1952), correcting 'Ethel' to 'Etta' -- and independently lists all three husbands (Mundell, Adolph, Strawn) on one page, which is the single piece of evidence the working tree had flagged as most important to find. F. John Rust's memorial carries a transcribed 1909 Athol Record obituary (with newspaper citation) giving his Illinois birth, 1896 arrival in Smith County, and 1893 marriage to Etta Rupert -- but it says only three children were born of that marriage, conflicting with the four children (Nina Frances, Mary Frances, Clarence Aison, William Bryan) Find A Grave links to the couple, an unresolved tension. On the German-vs-English question, this session found strong, specific evidence for German (East Frisian) origin: John Aissen Rust was born in Wybelsum and his wife Frauke Folkerts Everwyn Rust was born in Larrelt, both in Stadtkreis Emden, Lower Saxony (Ostfriesland), Germany, with Frauke dying in Pekin, Tazewell County, Illinois in 1885 -- establishing a Germany-to-Illinois-to-Kansas migration path and explaining the patronymic-style naming (Aissen, Folkerts, Everwyn, Meiner) in the family. Etta Rupert's own parents, George Clarence Rupert Sr and Emma J. Galbraith, were traced to Huntingdon/Blair County, Pennsylvania, with George dying at Athol, Smith County, Kansas -- showing both the Rust and Rupert sides converged on Smith County from different origins. William Bryan Rust's wife Mary Viola Beach Rust corroborates the old forum clue about a Rust brother's wife 'Viola.' All of this remains Find-A-Grave-level (a moderated, cross-referenced index/database of user-contributed and some newspaper-sourced content), not primary vital or census records, so the frames below prioritize pulling the actual 1900/1910 Smith County census and the pre-1913 county-level Smith County marriage registers next. A distinct, fully-transcribed Smith County 'Pleasant Hill Cemetery' (Martin Township, formerly Clemans Cemetery) was checked directly and contains no Rust burials, so it should not be conflated with the Reamsville Cemetery Rust cluster despite the similar name; a separate Find A Grave cemetery listing also named 'Pleasant Hill Cemetery in Reamsville' could not be conclusively confirmed as identical to or distinct from the Reamsville Cemetery entry that actually holds the Rust family memorials, and is flagged as unresolved rather than assumed."

### Wave 2

Working directly from wave 1's Find A Grave leads, I re-fetched the primary Find A Grave pages myself (browser-UA curl, not search snippets) for Mary Frances Rust Strawn, F. John Rust, Etta B. Rupert Rust Wells, Nina Frances Rust Utterback, Clarence Aison Rust, Walter William Mundell, John Aissen Rust, Ollie Rust Hartsook, and George Clarence Rupert Sr, independently confirming wave 1's core claim (Mary Frances's own memorial names both John F. Rust and Etta B. Rupert as parents and cross-links all three husbands) and adding several new, verified layers: a previously unreported half-sister, Ollie Rust Hartsook (1890-1982), confirmed as John Aissen Rust's daughter by his second wife Nomdje N. Meiner (m. 1885) -- explaining, but only partly (1 of ~6 siblings), the 1909 Athol Record obituary's claim that F. John Rust left 'four sisters and two brothers'; a sharper Rupert birthplace (Henderson Township, Huntingdon County, PA) and a documented 1911 remarriage by George Clarence Rupert Sr while his first wife lived on to 1929; and hard GPS coordinates for both candidate 'Pleasant Hill'/'Reamsville' cemetery listings (93320 vs 835833, ~3 miles apart), which sharpens but does not resolve the cemetery-identity question the predecessor flagged. Pushing one generation further on the German Rust line, an Ancestry.com crowd-sourced tree-match page for a Wybelsum-born 'Jan Aissen Rust' (1780-1830) lists a son 'Aisse Janssen Rust' (1815-1882) whose name and birth year fit East Frisia's alternating-patronymic convention as John Aissen Rust's (b.1842) likely father -- a genuinely new, but only compiled/speculative, lead requiring confirmation in the Wybelsum Ortsfamilienbuch. I was unable to access any primary 1900/1910 census image, the Kansas state census indexes (viewable only on-site at KSHS via Ancestry terminals), or the Smith County marriage register itself -- all remain the single highest-value next pulls, alongside a genuinely free but JS/browser-gated Kansas Historical Society newspaper portal (1854-1923) that could hold the Mundell-Rust wedding announcement.

### Wave 3

Final push on the RUST & RUPERT frontier resolved the German-vs-English question decisively in favor of German (East Frisian) origin, and pushed the confirmed Rust pedigree back to a man born in 1754 in Wybelsum, Ostfriesland. Re-fetching John Aissen Rust's own Find A Grave memorial (not seen directly by the predecessor) shows his birthplace recorded as Wybelsum, Stadtkreis Emden, Lower Saxony, Germany, born 12 Feb 1842, with a first wife Frauke Folkerts Everwyn Rust (1843-1885, m. 1867) who has never before appeared in this project's record -- and whose 1885 death lines up exactly with his 1885 remarriage to Nomdje Meiner. An independent, open, specialist East Frisian genealogy finding-aid (tote-punkte-ostfriesland.de, which indexes the published Wybelsum and Larrelt Ortssippenbuecher) contains a 'Jan Aissen Rust' born 12.2.1842 in Wybelsum, explicitly tagged as son of 'Aisse Janssen Rust,' who married in Germany on 12 March 1867 -- matching, to the day and to the year respectively, the Find A Grave birth and marriage data. That is two fully independent sources converging on the same exact birthdate and marriage year, which is strong (though still compiled, not primary-record) proof that the Kansas immigrant and the Wybelsum-born man are the same person, resolving what the predecessor could only frame as a naming-convention guess. The same finding-aid also independently corroborates the crowdsourced Ancestry tree-match for the next generation back: Aisse Janssen Rust (1815-1882) as son of Eisse/Eise Janssen Rust (b. 5 Mar 1754 Wybelsum, d. 19 Jan 1826) -- giving a five-generation, fully German (never English) surname chain: Eisse Janssen Rust (b.1754) > Jan Aissen Rust (1780-1830) > Aisse Janssen Rust (1815-1882) > John Aissen Rust (1842-1916, the Kansas immigrant) > F. John Rust (1870-1909, Mary Frances's father). Separately, F. John Rust's own memorial (also not previously fetched directly) states his parents as John Aissen Rust AND Frauke Folkerts Everwyn Rust (the first wife, not Nomdje) and tags Ollie Rust Hartsook as a 'Half Sibling' -- both facts new to this project. Cross-referencing this against the Reamsville Cemetery burial dates for Isaac J. Rust (b.1866), Martin J. Rust (b.1872), and Claus J. Rust (1879-1902), all of which fall inside the 1867-1885 first-marriage window, produces a new, coherent (but unconfirmed) hypothesis: these three are F. John Rust's full brothers by Frauke Folkerts Everwyn, which would make Isaac and Martin (both alive in 1909, unlike the already-deceased Claus) the 'two brothers' of the 1909 obituary, leaving the obituary's 'four sisters' as still-unidentified daughters of that same first marriage. On the cemetery-identity question the predecessor flagged as unresolved, this wave found decisive evidence: interment.net's own separate transcription of Pleasant Hill Cemetery (Find A Grave 93320) states it was originally called Clemans Cemetery, established 1888 from Isaac Clemans's land in Martin Township Section 20, and its full 142-name surname list contains zero Rust burials -- proving it is physically distinct from Reamsville Cemetery (Find A Grave 835833, Section 25, established 1900 from W.H. Farrs's land), which holds the entire Rust cluster. The Reamsville transcriber's own note that her cemetery is 'also known as Pleasant Hill Cemetery' appears to be a local mix-up, not evidence of shared ground. On the Rupert/Pennsylvania side, a directly re-fetched 1883 county history (J. Simpson Africa, History of Huntingdon and Blair Counties) documents a real Rupert family cluster holding township office in Henderson Township across the 1850s-1880s (David, Adam, Daniel, Joseph, and John Rupert) -- the exact decades and exact township where George Clarence Rupert Sr. (b.1855) grew up -- but no direct document ties him to any one of these men as father, and no ethnic-origin statement (German or otherwise) for the Rupert surname was found in that source, so the Rupert line's origin question remains genuinely open, unlike the now-resolved Rust question. The hard walls the predecessor identified (Kansas state census indexes locked to in-person KSHS/Ancestry terminals; FamilySearch record search rendering only a blank JS shell to unauthenticated fetches) were independently re-confirmed rather than cracked.
