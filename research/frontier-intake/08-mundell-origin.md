## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Rebuilt the Mundell line above Harvey William Mundell from open sources after the FamilySearch profile merge. Two independent compiled sources (OCCGS Civil War PDF and a Tuolumne County, CA historical-society genealogy site — NOT "St. Charles County," see correction below) agree: Harvey was born April 1837 in Clinton County, Indiana and died 26 March 1911 in Orange County, CA — this contradicts the task brief's "1821-1901" dates, which do not match any source found. Harvey's parents were John Mundell (b. c.1807/1808, Indiana) and Deborah Elmore (b. c.1820, Indiana; daughter of Providence Elmore + Lydia Elmore of Daviess Co., IN), married 31 Mar 1836 in Clinton County, Indiana. New this pass: two independently fetched Find A Grave compiled bios (sourced to census 1830-1900, an obituary, and a death certificate) name John Mundell's own parents as "James and Mary Mundell," pushing the open edge back one generation. A full sibling set for Harvey was recovered (Malinda, Johnathan, Solomon [Civil War, Co G Iowa 34th, matching Harvey's unit], Sarah Jane, Garret, Abner, Washington, Debry/Deborah, Stephen, John W.), and two of their obituaries were fetched directly (Sarah Jane Mundell Wilcox, Chariton Herald-Patriot, 26 Mar 1931; Rev. John W. Mundell, d.1949, Cainsville) confirming the family's migration Clinton Co., IN (to 1852) -> Jackson Twp., Lucas Co., IA -> Richland Twp., Putnam Co., MO (by 1880) -> a cluster around Princeton, Mercer Co., MO and Smith Co., KS by the 1930s. A parallel and very likely related family was found in Lucas County, Iowa: Joseph Mundell (b. Apr 1802 Kentucky, d. 8 Feb 1885, Union Twp., Lucas Co., IA), whose son James R. Mundell was born 1833 in Clinton County, Indiana and moved to the same Jackson Township, Lucas County, Iowa in 1849 — three years before John Mundell's family arrived in the same township. This is a strong but unproven candidate-brother lead. A large compiled Greene County, Pennsylvania Mundell genealogy ("Descendants of Abner M. Mundell," traced to New Castle Co., Delaware colonial roots) was recovered via the Wayback Machine after its host site (mundellpa.tripod.com) went dead; it independently documents another Mundell household also in Jackson Township, Clinton County, Indiana in 1849, and the site itself frames the family as having "Irish roots" while a quoted 1931 family letter within the same compiled book claims Welsh origin, and general surname reference works (SurnameDB) trace "Mundell" to a Norman-derived Scottish/Dumfriesshire surname. None of the three origin claims (Irish, Welsh, Scottish/Dumfriesshire) is backed by a primary immigration record in what was found — this is a genuinely open, three-way-contested question, not a solved one. No direct paper-trail link was established between the Greene Co. PA Mundells and the Clinton Co. IN John Mundell family; it is a strong circumstantial coincidence (same surname, same specific township) requiring land/probate or Y-DNA confirmation. Ancestry.com, WikiTree's genealogy hub page, and Geni.com all blocked automated access (bot-detection/WAF) during this pass and should be revisited from an authenticated session.

### Wave 2

Pushed one full generation above John Mundell (m. Deborah Elmore, 1836, Clinton Co., IN) using free Indiana county-history full text and census indexes, and found a well-corroborated candidate father: a James Mundell, native of Kentucky, who made one of the earliest (1816) land entries in Pleasant Run Township, Lawrence County, Indiana, appears as head of household on the 1820 Lawrence Co. census and the 1830 Clinton Co. census (as "Mondell"), and moved his family to Jackson Township, Clinton County by 1828/1829 -- the same township, same decade, where John Mundell's own family is independently documented (John served on the Clinton Co. grand jury in April 1832, four years before his marriage). Two separate published Clinton County histories (Chicago, 1886, and Bowen/Claybaugh, 1913) each carry a full biographical sketch of a documented son of a "James Mundell": Adam Mundell (b.1812, Lawrence Co.) and George Mundell (b.1848, Jackson Twp.). The 1886 sketch is the richest origin find of this pass: it states outright that Adam's father "James Mundell was a native of Kentucky, and of Irish descent" and that his mother "Mary (Smith) Mundell was a native of Germany" -- the first primary-type published origin claim tied directly to the Clinton County cluster (rather than to the unconnected Greene County, PA family), plus a new German-immigrant maternal claim. I also resolved one internal contradiction in the predecessor's OCCGS source: its 1850-census citation placing the family in "Doyle, Clarke County, Iowa" uses a microfilm roll number from the 1860-census series (M653, not the 1850 series M432 the heading claims), and is directly contradicted by the Find A Grave bio (independently re-fetched and quoted this pass) which places the family in Jackson Township, Clinton County, Indiana on the same 1850 census -- the Iowa citation should be treated as an OCCGS transcription error, not a real residence. I also re-confirmed, by fetching the page directly, that "stchsgenealogy.com" is the Southern Tuolumne Co. Historical Society (Groveland, CA), not "St. Charles County" as named in the task brief. A targeted fresh search for any independent corroboration of a "Harvey Mundell, 1821-1901" turned up nothing except an unrelated "Harvey Mitchell (1821-1901)," reinforcing that the task brief's 1821-1901 dates are very likely a FamilySearch tree-merge artifact, not a distinct real person. Finally, re-reading the Greene County, PA "Descendants of Abner M. Mundell" PDF directly (recovered again via Wayback CDX listing) turned up a third, previously unreported Mundell household captured in that same document -- Hugh Mundell's family, whose son Benjamin L. Mundell was born in Jackson Township, Clinton County, Indiana in 1849 on "the James Sr. homestead" -- meaning at least three distinct, surname-sharing Mundell households (John's, the newly-confirmed James Sr./Adam/George line, and Hugh's PA-descended line) all passed through the same one Indiana township in the same narrow decade with no document yet linking any two of them genealogically. I also found that the PA document's own rigorous 1968 genealogy (pension and church records) names Abner M. Mundell's father as "James and his wife Margaret (Garrett) Mundle" with no stated origin country, directly at odds with a separately-quoted, internally garbled 1931 family letter elsewhere in the same PDF that claims a Welsh-born ancestor while misattributing to him a wife's name that actually belonged to a person three generations later -- this weakens, rather than strengthens, the Welsh claim the predecessor flagged as open. Net effect: the origin question is sharper, not resolved -- the immediate Clinton County line has its first primary-type "Irish descent" claim, but no immigration, naturalization, or original land-patent record has yet been pulled to test it, and the James Mundell Sr.-to-John Mundell parent-child link is still inferential (same surname/township/decade, not a named-heir document).

### Wave 3

This pass pushed the Mundell research primarily through primary-source triangulation rather than new generations. The single biggest gain was pulling James Mundell's actual federal land patents from BLM GLO Records (the government's own digitized primary records, previously only referenced secondhand): they show a documented Kentucky-to-Indiana migration path of Lawrence County (1816, 1820) -> a previously unknown intermediate stop in Fountain County (1828) -> Clinton County (1830-31), and reveal a tight cluster of other same-surname landholders (Andrew, William, Joshua, Isaiah, Joseph, plus a bounty-warrant patent for a person literally named "Elmore Mundell") entering adjoining or nearby tracts in the same decade -- strong circumstantial evidence of one extended family, though no patent names a father-son relationship outright. Re-fetching the underlying 1886 and 1913 Clinton County histories directly from archive.org (rather than trusting the predecessor's paraphrase) confirmed the "Irish descent"/Mary Smith-of-Germany quote verbatim, confirmed "John Mundell" by name in the original 1832 grand-jury list, and surfaced a new, previously unreported "Isaiah Mundell" (father of a Mary Mundell who married Samuel Lyon) independently corroborated by his own 1837 Lawrence County land patent. The most significant new material came from Find A Grave: an independently-compiled researcher's page for John Mundell states outright that he was born to "James and Mary Mundell" around 1808 in Indiana -- converging with, though not independently sourcing, the 1886 book's James-and-Mary(Smith) couple -- and a matching page for Deborah Ann Elmore Mundell supplies her parents (Providence and Lydia Elmore), a full census trail from Daviess County through Clinton County, Lucas County IA, to Putnam County, Missouri, and names all ten of John and Deborah's children, while candidly stating their actual death date and place remain unknown. A separate 1886 Goodspeed county history independently corroborates Providence Elmore's father Isaac Elmore's 1818 Daviess County land entry. Harvey Mundell's own Find A Grave memorial, sourced to a Civil War grave-registration project, gives his correct dates (b.1837, d.1911, Orange Co. California) and confirms the task brief's "1821-1901" is indeed a tree-merge artifact. On access: WikiTree is now hard-blocked by an AWS WAF JavaScript challenge even with browser headers and retries, and FamilySearch's record-search (as opposed to ancestors-tree) interface is confirmed to require login. No Kentucky-side record (tax, marriage bond, land grant, or will) naming any Mundell was located this pass despite targeted searches of five candidate counties, and no immigration, naturalization, or colonial-era record was found for any Mundell generation earlier than the Kentucky-native James Mundell Sr. himself -- meaning the "final push" toward emigration origins genuinely dead-ends at Kentucky until his specific county of residence there is identified (the Jeffersonville, Indiana land office that processed both his Lawrence County entries points toward the Louisville/Jefferson County, KY area as the most promising starting point) and a will, deed, or church record from that county names his own father.
