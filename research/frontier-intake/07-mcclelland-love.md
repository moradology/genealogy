## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Working from the two known Gen-5 compiled leads already in index.html (Alexander M. McClelland + Sarah Hoge; Thomas Louis Love + Hannah Ann James), I fetched the underlying WikiTree and Find a Grave pages directly (curl with browser UA, since both sites block default clients) and cross-checked them against the free genealogytrails.com transcription of the Athens County OH marriage-record index. Several new, checkable facts emerged that are not yet in index.html: (1) a previously unidentified son, Pvt. Isaiah H. McClelland (1833-1862), who enlisted in Co. B, 75th OVI at Athens OH in Dec. 1861, died of typhoid in Virginia during Stonewall Jackson's Valley Campaign, and whose widow's 1865 remarriage in Troy, KS lines up exactly with the family obituary's stated 1865 move to Kansas; (2) direct Athens Co marriage-index hits confirming two McClelland daughters' married surnames (Neiswanger, Dew) that match the Find a Grave sibling list, plus three unmatched McClellan brides (Harriet, Mary Jane, Sarah) that may account for some of the "eleven children" the mother's obituary says she had, of whom only 3 sons were alive in 1892; (3) a full Iowa way-station for the Love family and for Dorsey McClelland's own household - Thomas L. Love and Hannah (James) Love both died in 1871 in Fairfield, Jefferson County, Iowa, and Dorsey's daughters Maude (b. Athens Co OH 1857) and twins Cora/Ora (b. Fairfield, Jefferson Co IA 1872) trace an Ohio-to-Iowa-to-Kansas migration not previously mapped. Everything found remains compiled/secondary (WikiTree profiles, Find a Grave contributor bios, an index transcription); no marriage license, census image, probate file, or pension file has been directly viewed. A search for Alexander M. McClelland's actual grave (Bishopville Cemetery) came up empty, and no free index yet located reaches back far enough to directly document the ~1822 PA marriage or the ~1828 Ohio marriage. Five research frames are provided below, each with jurisdiction stacks, concrete record-set URLs (with paywalls flagged), and prioritized next pulls.

### Wave 2

Working from the predecessor's wave-1 leads, I re-fetched the same three core WikiTree profiles (Sarah Hoge McClelland, Alexander M. McClelland, Lovina Love McClelland) directly and found that two of them carry much deeper, previously unreported ancestor tables that the predecessor's report never mentioned: Sarah Hoge's line pushes back through Thomas Hogue (1765-1837, Waynesburg, Greene Co. PA) to George Hoge (1733-1805, Botetourt Co., VA) and, unsourced, all the way to William Hoge Sr. and Barbara Hume, the traditional Scots immigrant couple linked to Opequon Church near Winchester, VA; Hannah Ann James's line pushes back through Samuel James and Mary Pugsley of Shapleigh, York Co., Maine to Elisha James of Somersworth, NH and John James/Anne Lord (b. 1718), meaning the James family's roots are colonial New England, not Ohio. Neither chain carries a source citation on WikiTree, but I found a partially independent, 1969-manuscript-based rootsweb tree (via Wayback Machine) that corroborates George Hoge's birth date and Blackledge marriage while conflicting on his death year/place -- a real, checkable discrepancy, not mere repetition. Separately, I ran down the predecessor's Isaiah H. McClelland (75th OVI) lead against two free, non-Find-a-Grave primary-ish sources: Ohio's official 1886 state roster (archive.org, OCR'd but directly read) confirms Co. B, 75th OVI and supplies an exact death date and place (28 Mar 1862, Franklin, Va./W.Va, 'of disease') that sharpens the predecessor's vaguer 'typhoid during the Valley Campaign' claim, and a DPLA catalog record independently confirms his widow's pension file (Ruth Tharp McClelland, Application WC5670) without relying on any Find a Grave bio. I also fetched Sarah Hoge McClellan's Find a Grave memorial directly and caught a new, small but real discrepancy: the obituary it hosts says she was buried in 'the Troy Cemetery,' while the memorial itself is filed under 'Mount Olive Cemetery.' Checking genealogytrails.com's Athens County, OH marriage index directly established that this free transcription only covers 1851 onward, so it structurally cannot resolve the ~1828 Love-James marriage -- an important negative result for the frame. Attempts to verify the predecessor's Iowa way-station claim (Thomas/Hannah Love dying 1871 in Fairfield, Jefferson Co., IA) and to resolve the John Allison McClellan death-date conflict were unsuccessful this pass. WikiTree also proved to rate-limit heavily mid-session, blocking several planned individual-ancestor-profile pulls (Hogue-306, Hoge-121/51/6, the James- profiles) that should be retried with wider spacing.

### Wave 3

Building on the predecessor's wave, this pass independently re-fetched the core WikiTree Hoge profiles (Hoge-395, Hogue-306) before the site's rate limiter shut down all further WikiTree access for the remainder of the session (a harder, longer block than the predecessor experienced), and used that window plus Find A Grave, a financial-advisor's posted Hoge family history, a Wayback Machine snapshot of a rootsweb Hoge genealogy, and an Iowa county genealogical society's index to materially sharpen -- and in several places genuinely complicate -- the picture. Key advances: (1) confirmed directly from Sarah Hoge's own WikiTree page that her only actual citation is a Find A Grave memorial, with the entire deep ancestor chain unsourced, but also recovered her full, internally-consistent 1792-1815 sibling list, including a second sister (Hannah Hoge) who also married a McClellan -- a new, unexplored lead against the Alexander M. McClelland parentage dead end; (2) found that George Hoge's own Find A Grave memorial says he was born in Loudoun County, VA and died in Greene County, PA, directly conflicting with the predecessor's reported 'Botetourt Co. VA' and grounding the Greene Co. PA residence story (Fort Bell/Ruff Creek) in real, independently-confirmed local history; (3) fetched a fresh, fuller copy of the rootsweb 'Gary Hogue' manuscript tree, which matches WikiTree almost exactly on dates for George Hoge and Thomas Hoge but lists an entirely different, non-overlapping set of children for Thomas Hoge and omits Sarah altogether -- a real unresolved gap, and notably does NOT contain the 1794-PA-death claim the predecessor attributed to it, suggesting either page drift or a misattribution; (4) in Jefferson County, Iowa, found a county genealogical society index entry for 'Thomas L. Love 1795-1870' with Hannah James marked 'wife #2' -- the first non-Find-a-Grave, non-WikiTree corroboration of the Ohio-to-Iowa Love/James thread, but it both shifts the death year and reveals a previously-unknown first wife; (5) re-confirmed, via a second differently-titled genealogytrails.com page, that the Athens Co. OH marriage transcription's real coverage starts in 1851 despite claiming 1803-1900 in its title; (6) independently re-extracted the Ohio Civil War roster's garbled OCR table for Isaiah H. McClelland, confirming the Franklin/Staunton place-of-death ambiguity is real and not a misreading, found a plausible (not proven) brother-candidate Thomas J. McClelland enlisted three days later in the same company, and confirmed via Find A Grave that John Allison McClellan's memorial supports 23 Jan 1903 with no obituary explaining the competing 3 June 1903 date. The Hannah Ann James Maine/New Hampshire chain could not be advanced at all this pass (WikiTree blocked throughout, and the one authoritative compiled reference for that region, the Genealogical Dictionary of Maine and New Hampshire, is access-restricted on Internet Archive) -- it remains exactly where the predecessor left it: a wholly unverified frontier. Where lines truly end without further paid/in-person records: Alexander M. McClelland's parents (confirmed still blank on WikiTree), George Hoge's actual VA birthplace (three sources disagree, no primary VA record found under any county), the identity of Thomas L. Love's first wife (Iowa family-sheet notation only), and Isaiah H. McClelland's exact death location (needs a clean page image or his pension file, not further OCR).
