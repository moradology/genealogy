## Wave syntheses (verbatim from the agents)

### Wave 1

Antonette Grams Zodrow's Find a Grave memorial links to a separate, previously unexamined memorial for her mother, Rosa (Rolof) Grams (48431935), which independently fetching (via country-subdomain workarounds for Find a Grave's Cloudflare gate) revealed a transcribed 1895 obituary from The Sheridan County Democrat (Hoxie, Kansas). That obituary names Rosa's father as Antonius Roelofs (a Rolof/Roloff variant satisfying the requested name-variant search), gives her husband as Johann Grams (with an internally inconsistent 'b. 1877' birth note that looks like a transcription error), records her death on 30 Mar 1895 at the Sheridan Township, Sheridan County, Kansas home of son-in-law John Zodrow, her 1875 immigration to America at about age 73, her burial at Calvary Cemetery in Leoville, Decatur County (the same cemetery as Antonette and John Zodrow), and -- most useful for the frontier -- states she left four surviving daughters in 1895: two in Germany, one in Wisconsin, and Antonette in Kansas. Independent BLM General Land Office records confirm John Zodrow held a 1885 Homestead Act patent in Republic County, Kansas (Concordia land office) and a 1900 Timber Culture Act patent in Sheridan County, Kansas (Colby land office), corroborating the family's Republic-to-Sheridan County move already suggested in secondary compiled sources (Geni, which itself is Incapsula-blocked and could not be directly quoted, so its Newton/Marquette County Wisconsin 1870-census and 1880-census claims are flagged as unverified leads, not confirmed findings). A free-to-browse (but login-walled at the detail level) Deutsch Krone Project surname index turned up a documented Grams presence directly at Stara Lubianka (modern name for Lebehnke, the Zodrow family's already-hypothesized home village) and a Roloff/Rohloff surname cluster centered on Sypniewo, Dzikowo, and Walcz itself (Walcz = Deutsch Krone town) -- promising but unconfirmed regional corroboration for the 'Deutsch Krone area' guess in the prompt. No independent Grams or Roloff burial besides Rosa's and Antonette's was found at the Leoville cemetery, and a large Grams cluster located in Marquette County, Wisconsin appears to be a separate Lutheran-affiliated family, not obviously the Catholic Grams line being researched here.

### Wave 2

Re-verified the predecessor's Rosa (Rolof) Grams Find a Grave memorial (48431935) directly via a fresh curl fetch and confirmed its exact wording, then pushed further on the open questions. Key new results: (1) an independent ksgenweb.org local-history page names 'John Zodrow' among ~30 original 1885 pioneer settlers of Leoville, Decatur Co., Kansas -- a source wholly separate from Find a Grave/Geni that corroborates the family's Kansas settlement chronology. (2) Leoville's Catholic parish is officially 'Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin Mary Parish,' founded 1885 (confirmed on the Diocese of Salina's own site) -- a plausible explanation for why Rosa's 1895 obituary calls her burial ground 'St. Mary cemetery' while Find a Grave's structured field says 'Calvary Cemetery, Leoville' (same Marian-dedicated parish ground, different name registers), though this remains a hypothesis, not documentary proof of identity. (3) A direct, live Find a Grave surname search of the entire state of Kansas returns zero 'Johann Grams' memorials and only Rosa's own record under 'Grams' (plus two unrelated 20th-century Charles Grams), hardening the predecessor's tentative negative finding that Johann Grams (Rosa's husband) left no traceable American grave. (4) Querying the Deutsch Krone Project's actual person-search index (not just its place-search, which the predecessor could not get past) turned up three Grams entries at Stara Lubianka, including 'Wilhelmine Friederike Grams, b. 1833, Stara Lubianka' -- a generationally plausible candidate for one of the two Grams daughters the 1895 obituary says were still living in Germany -- and confirmed the Roloff surname index for Sypniewo/Dzikowo/Walcz contains a 'Rosalia Roloff, b. About 1805' entry with no recorded village, the single closest name-and-birth-year match to Rosa herself found anywhere in that database, alongside undated 'Johann Roloff b. c.1790' and 'Laurenz Roloff b. c.1800' entries -- all three login-walled at the detail level. A parallel query for the literal surname 'Roelofs' returns zero results, supporting that this is an anglicized rendering of Roloff rather than the parish-register spelling. (5) A full, page-by-page Find a Grave search of Marquette County, Wisconsin turned up all 26 extant 'Grams'/'Gramse' memorials and every single one sits in a Lutheran-affiliated or non-denominational cemetery (Zion Evangelical Lutheran/Neshkoro, St. John Lutheran/Budsin, St. Paul Lutheran/Newton, Emmanuel Lutheran/Mecan) -- strengthening, with complete rather than partial data, the predecessor's hunch that this is an unrelated Lutheran Grams family. (6) A distinct, unrelated Find a Grave memorial for a 'John Zodrow Sr. (1844-1912)' independently states he 'was born in Lebehnke, West Prussia (East Germany),' married Agnes Mielke, and emigrated via Bremen on the ship Weser in 1887 to Naperville, Illinois -- not the target ancestor, but a second, independent witness that Lebehnke was a real Zodrow home village, corroborating (without proving) the existing Sabanka-to-Lebehnke hypothesis already in the site. (7) Geni remains fully Incapsula-blocked and could not be verified; a WebSearch-tool synthesis that surfaced specific-sounding claims (an SS Allemannia August 1868 arrival, a '26-year-old John Zudro' 1870 Newton-township census entry) could NOT be corroborated against any fetched primary source this session (ISTG's transcribed 1868 Allemannia voyages cover only Feb/May/Jun, not Aug) and must be treated as unverified, not as a finding. FamilySearch record search, the BLM GLO patent search UI, and Chronicling America's page images are all JS/Cloudflare-gated and could not be queried by curl in this session; the newspaper's existence and 1892-1896 run (matching the obituary's 1895 citation) was confirmed only at the catalog-metadata level via Library of Congress search-result titles, not against the actual page scan.

### Wave 3

Re-fetched Rosa (Rolof) Grams's Find a Grave memorial (48431935) and Antonette Grams Zodrow's memorial (156365204) directly and confirmed the obituary's full text, surfacing one previously under-flagged detail with high leverage: Rosa's 1895 obituary names FOUR daughters, not two -- two in Germany, ONE IN WISCONSIN (unnamed, married surname unknown), and Antonette in Kansas. This reframes the prior wave's negative "26 Grams/Gramse burials in Marquette County WI, all Lutheran" finding as likely uninformative rather than disproof: since Rosa's obituary names only daughters, any Wisconsin-based relative would carry a married surname, never "Grams," so a Grams-surname cemetery search there could never find her regardless of whether the family really passed through that county. An independent KSGenWeb cemetery-transcription re-pull gave Rosa's precise birth date (7 Feb 1802, not just "1802"), internally consistent with her stated age at death. A full (not place-filtered) Deutsch Krone Project surname search for "Grams" turned up 210 tree-wide entries including seven distinct "Johann Grams" candidates (b. c1787-1817) plus an unnamed "Farmer" Grams (b. c1807) -- any could be Rosa's husband, but the project's detail/location data is confirmed to sit behind a $42/year paid subscription, not merely a free login, so none is resolvable this session. A parallel full "Roloff" search surfaced a previously unreported "Rosa Roloff" (b. 1834, Sypniewo, Kreis Flatow) and "Christina Roloff" (b. 1811, an ID adjacent to the already-known Johann Roloff b. c1790), pointing to a Roloff family cluster at Sypniewo -- a county (Flatow) directly adjacent to the Zodrow line's existing Deutsch Krone target. A fresh Kartenmeister pull confirms Sypniewo had its own Catholic parish, Lutheran parish, and civil registry, with the Catholic church attested there since 1789. Fresh nationwide and Kansas-wide Find a Grave searches for "Johann/John Grams" and for "Rolof/Roloff/Rohloff/Roelofs" surnames found no plausible match for Rosa's husband or father anywhere traceable, hardening (with appropriate caveats about Find a Grave's sparse Prussian coverage) the working theory that Johann Grams died in Prussia before 1875 and left no American or documented-online grave. Wisconsin Historical Society vital records, BLM GLO, Chronicling America, WikiTree, FamilySearch ancestors pages, and castlegarden.org were all confirmed inaccessible via curl this session (JS-rendered, WAF-challenged, Cloudflare-challenged, or unresponsive) -- explicitly flagged as access gates, not evidence of absence. The trail runs out, on current free/open sources, at: (1) Rosa's own pre-1875 Prussian origin and marriage to Johann Grams, blocked by the Deutsch Krone Project paywall and by pre-1874 Prussian civil-registration gaps; and (2) the identity and married name of Rosa's Wisconsin-based daughter, blocked by JS-gated Wisconsin vital-record and census search interfaces. The record that would most directly extend the line is a Wisconsin pre-1907 marriage-index hit for a Grams-born bride (Wisconsin Historical Society, browser access) and a Sypniewo or Lebehnke Catholic parish death/marriage register predating 1874 (Szukaj w Archiwach or Geneteka, both free but not yet located for this specific parish)."
