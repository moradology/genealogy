# 1880 Census Index Search for the Rust Upstream Household

## Question

Does the 1880 federal census index expose a household that directly connects John F. Rust, born about 1870, to the proposed father John Aissen Rust and clarifies whether Nomdje was the son's mother or a later wife?

## Working Hypothesis

John F. Rust may have been enumerated with John Aissen Rust in or near Smith County, Kansas, but the relationship cannot be upgraded from the current lead without a linking household or equivalent primary record. Frauke Folkerts Everwyn remains the proposed biological mother, while Nomdje must be treated separately unless a record states her relationship to the son.

## Why It Is Plausible

- John F. Rust's later Smith County household and life chronology are established independently by the 1900, 1905, and 1910 censuses already attached to `case.06`.
- The Reamsville cemetery cluster places F. John Rust near John Aissen Rust and Nomdje, making a local family relationship plausible but not self-proving.
- A direct 1880 household would be especially valuable because that census normally states each household member's relationship to the head.

## What Is Not Proved

- Searches in collection 6742 for John Rust born about 1870, F. John Rust born about 1870, and John Rust born about 1842 returned no Kansas candidate on the first indexed results page.
- The rare-name search for Nomdje Rust returned unrelated results and was rejected by the cockpit rather than cached as a valid name match.
- One result named a John F. Rust born about 1870, but he was in Hopkins County, Kentucky, and identified as a son of Lorenzo D.; geography and father therefore independently exclude him.
- These index negatives do not establish census absence. A mistranscribed surname, a different 1880 residence, an omitted household, or a result beyond the exposed page remains possible.
- No searched record named Frauke Folkerts Everwyn, and no result proved either proposed parent-child relationship.

## Next Moves

1. Search the 1870 federal census using the older generation and surname variants, allowing for a pre-Kansas residence.
2. Pull Kansas state-census records after 1880 for the Reamsville/Smith County cluster and look for a stated son or adjacent household.
3. Search Smith County probate, guardianship, cemetery, and newspaper records for John Aissen Rust and both wives.
4. Treat any future candidate as unlinked until a household, probate, or vital record connects the people; reject same-name candidates after two independent mismatches.
