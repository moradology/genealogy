# Haden and Reid Households in the 1850 and 1860 Censuses

## Question

Do original census indexes corroborate the compiled claim that Elizabeth J. Haden belonged to the Andrew Jackson Haden and Sophia Reid household, and does the reported older Robert Reid actually share their 1860 household?

## Working Hypothesis

The A. J. and Sophia Haden household in Cumberland County is the proposed ancestral family, with the age-consistent E. Haden in 1850 representing Elizabeth J. Haden. Robert Reid/Ried is a real older household associate in 1860, but his exact relationship to Sophia is not stated.

## Why It Is Plausible

- The 1850 record places A. J. Haden, born about 1816 in Virginia, and Sophia, about 33, together in Cumberland County dwelling and family 823.
- The household sequence includes E. Haden at about age eight, consistent with Elizabeth J. Haden's circa-1843 birth, alongside several other younger Hadens.
- In 1860 A. J. Haden, about 45 and Virginia-born, and Sophia, about 43, remain in Cumberland County, now in dwelling and family 389.
- Robert Ried, about 69 and Kentucky-born, is separately indexed in that exact 1860 dwelling and family number, independently confirming the older Reid associate described by the compiled source.
- The repeated names, ages, birthplaces, and county provide continuity between the two Haden households.

## What Is Not Proved

- The 1850 and 1860 schedules do not state household relationships. Co-residence and age support the proposed family structure but do not document it directly.
- The cockpit's 1850 household extraction shifts several cells because there is no relationship column. The sequence and age of E. Haden are usable, but the remaining rows should not be silently normalized without the original schedule.
- No census field identifies E. Haden by the full later name Elizabeth J. Haden or connects her to her later spouse.
- Robert Ried's shared dwelling proves association, not that he was Sophia's father; brother, uncle, other relative, or non-relative remain possible.
- The upstream Richard Haden and Sarah Lewis Tompkins parentage was not tested by these records.

## Next Moves

1. Pull Elizabeth J. Haden's marriage application, marriage return, or family Bible evidence for an explicit parent statement.
2. Search Cumberland County probate, deeds, tax, and marriage records for Robert Reid/Ried and Sophia Reed/Reid.
3. Compare the 1860 household with Sophia's reported 1870 household and the Edwin Reid associate, preserving Reed, Reid, and Ried variants.
4. Keep the relationship tier inferential until a record states the relationship; do not convert co-residence into direct parentage.
