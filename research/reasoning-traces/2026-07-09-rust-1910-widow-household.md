# The Widow's Boarder: Frances Rust and Walter Mundell in 1910

First research thread driven end-to-end through the cockpit write path: status ->
show case.06 -> ancestry goto/open (agent `tester`) -> evidence add (pull 27,
validated + attested through `./gen`) -> this trace via `trace new`.

## Question

Case.06 had Mary Frances Rust's parentage as a candidate chain only: the 1900
and 1905 censuses show John F. Rust + Ethel with exactly three children
(Francis, Clarence, Willie), but nothing yet tied OUR Mary Frances — the woman
who married Walter Mundell at Smith Center in 1910 and later became Frances
Adolph — into that household. John F. Rust died 18 Oct 1909, so the April 1910
census was the predicted decisive record: if Frances appears in a widowed Ethel
household, the parentage link closes; if she appears already married to Walter,
the marriage corroborates instead.

## What the record shows

1910 U.S. census, Martin Township, Smith County, Kansas (ED 0197, sheet 8A):

- Etta Rust, 34, widowed, Head — Ethel B. Rupert Rust, months after John F.'s death
- Francis Rust, 15, Daughter — Mary Frances, b. ~1894-95 Kansas
- Clarence Rust, 14, Son; William Rust, 12, Son — the exact 1900/1905 sibling set
- **Walter Mundell, 23, Boarder**

Both predicted outcomes landed in one record: Frances is the widow's daughter
(the case.06 parentage link), AND her future husband is boarding in the same
house in the year of their marriage. The courtship is visible in the census —
the young farmhand boarding with a widow's family married the daughter at Smith
Center within months.

## What this settles

- Mary Frances Rust belongs to the John F. Rust + Ethel B. Rupert household of
  Martin Township: same township, same widow, same three-child set, right age.
  Case.06's core question is now census-evidenced at the person level.
- The Walter William Mundell + Mary Frances Rust marriage context (M-4·5) gains
  a primary record; this household is the hinge between the Rust chain
  (M-10·11 and the East Frisian line above it) and the Mundell line.

## What Is Not Proved

- Francis's father is recorded as born in Illinois and mother in Pennsylvania.
  The Reamsville cluster's John F. Rust descends from East Frisian immigrants;
  an Illinois birthplace for him needs reconciling (informant error, or a
  detail that refines which John F. Rust this is). Flagged, not silently
  resolved.
- The 1910 Smith Center marriage record itself (Walter + Frances) has not been
  pulled; it remains the direct document for the marriage date and, possibly,
  parents' names.
