# David and Virginia Durham Census Continuity, 1860-1880

## Question

Do the competing profiles for David Monroe Durham represent two men, or can one household be traced continuously through the 1860, 1870, and 1880 censuses despite reported birth and death differences?

## Working Hypothesis

The 1860, 1870, and 1880 entries describe one David and Virginia Durham household. The circa-1829 versus circa-1832/1834 birth-year variation and the North Carolina versus Virginia birthplace variation are census-reporting conflicts, while the separate 1887 and 1897 death claims remain unadjudicated.

## Why It Is Plausible

- In 1860 David Durham, 26 and North Carolina-born, appears in Muhlenberg County with Virginia, America E., and Nancy A.
- In 1870 David Durham, 38 and North Carolina-born, remains in Muhlenberg County with Virginia A. and an age-progressing child indexed as Marica L., followed by Nancy A., Mary T., Patrick M., and Milton.
- In 1880 D. M. Durham, 51 and Virginia-born, heads a household in nearby Nebo with wife Virginia A., 37, and children Mary I., P. M., D. M., Louisa, Nellie, and Lilian.
- Virginia's reported age advances exactly from 17 to 27 to 37, and the younger children progress coherently across census years.
- The 1870 `Marica L.` reading is age-consistent with the 1860 America E. entry. It is retained as an index conflict rather than silently corrected.
- Among the search results, the target households are uniquely linked by Virginia's name, the children's sequence, North Carolina/Virginia origins, and the Muhlenberg-to-Hopkins geography.

## What Is Not Proved

- David's reported age changes from an implied birth about 1834 to 1832 to 1829, and his birthplace changes from North Carolina to Virginia.
- The 1860 and 1870 household extractors shift some row cells; ambiguous child ages and spellings were not normalized beyond what the structured fields support.
- The censuses end in 1880 and therefore cannot decide between the reported 1887 and 1897 deaths.
- No record in this thread names David's parents, so `gap.durham.david.parents` remains open.
- The 1857 Illinois marriage date was not independently tested in this thread, although the family is already in Kentucky by 1860.

## Next Moves

1. Pull the original cemetery or burial record behind each death date and compare spouse, residence, and age.
2. Search Hopkins and Muhlenberg County probate, estate, deed, and newspaper records for a death window from 1887 through 1897.
3. Use any death or probate record that identifies kin to open a parentage search; do not attach parents from the shared name alone.
4. Preserve the 1829/1832/1834 and North Carolina/Virginia discrepancies in future transcriptions until a stronger vital record resolves them.
