# Long line: Chapman/Fulton/Goodspeed/Chalkley validation (2026-07-08)

Independent validation of the frontier-intake Long cluster (`research/frontier-intake/06-long.md`,
research/archive/program-board-2026-07-08.md 3.6). Every source below was re-fetched from archive.org full-text OCR and
quote-matched verbatim by a validation agent on 2026-07-08; verdicts and the quotes
as matched are recorded here. Ledger entries for these four sources are queued for
the next index.html landing window (they will receive src ids then; URLs cited
directly in the interim).

## Verdicts

1. **CONFIRMED — Chapman, History of Pike County, Illinois (1880), John F. Long sketch**
   (Montezuma Township, p. 462; https://archive.org/details/historyofpikecou00chap)
   > "John F. Long, farmer, sec. 32; P. O. Milton; was born in Tennessee in 1805, son of
   > Robert and Betsy (Wasson) Long, natives of North Carolina, both of whom have died.
   > The subject of this sketch came to this county in Oct., 1828, and settled west of
   > Milton... He was married in Vanderburgh county, Ind., Feb. 23, 1826, to Miss Sally
   > A. Patton, a native of that county, where she was born in 1809. To them have been
   > born 8 children, 7 of whom are living, Phoebe J., Robert N., William H., James H.,
   > Samuel A., Mary E. and Sarah M."
   Sally A. Patton was born 1809 in Vanderburgh County (native, not just married there).
   Load-bearing detail: Chapman names only one wife for John F. Long.

2. **CONFIRMED — Fulton, History of Jefferson County, Iowa (1912-14), vol. 1**
   (https://archive.org/details/historyofjeffers01fult)
   Abingdon Christian Church organized by Rev. Robert Long **in 1843** (fixed by the
   preceding sentence's "In 1843..."; the compiled "1844" is wrong):
   > "In the same year a Christian Church with fifteen members was organized at
   > Abingdon by Rev. Robert Long, its first preacher."
   Polk Township formed 6 Jan 1845; judges named:
   > "James B. Davis, Isaac Campbell and Robert Long were named as the judges."
   CAVEAT: Fulton does NOT state Davis was Robert Long's stepson — that link is
   compiled-tier only. Write "alongside James B. Davis", never "with his stepson".
   Poll list shows "41. Robert Long" and "52. William K. Long"; the list is likely
   1848 (Polk Twp did not exist until Jan 1845), not 1844 as one wave reported.

3. **CONFIRMED — Goodspeed, History of Posey County, Indiana (1886)**
   (https://archive.org/details/cu31924028803406, p. 408-409)
   > "The logs were bought of Robert Long... Robert Long is believed to have built the
   > first mill in the west part of town."
   > "Rev. Joseph Wason, dissenting from these views, organized a faction and withdrew
   > from the church. They organized as a separate body near 1816... usually been known
   > as the Christian Body, New Lights or 'Wasonites."
   Goodspeed does not personally tie Robert Long to the Wason split; the sect and the
   Liberty locus are documented as claimed.

4. **CONFIRMED — Chalkley, Chronicles of the Scotch-Irish Settlement in Virginia, vol. 2**
   (https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_wk8PAAAAYAAJ)
   Joseph Long's will, 11 Sep 1757, proved 16 Nov 1757, names sons Samuel, Henry,
   Joseph (infant), John, wife Anna:
   > "To son Samuel, tract at the Welch cabins; to son Henry, tract near place called
   > Billar Spring; to son Joseph, infant, tract where testator lives; to son John,
   > tract at the Big Spring known as Island Ford."
   Buffalo Creek deed, Samuel Long to John Long, 15 acres, £100, teste Wm. Davis,
   James Young, Edward Paris — the printed year is OCR-lost ("17—"); 1764 is a
   contextual read from adjacent entries (10 Nov 1764 / 6 Nov 1764). **Cite as
   "[1764]".** The Samuel→Robert parent-child step remains UNPROVEN (case.13).

5. **CONFIRMED with a corrected reading — Chapman, Thomas S. Long of Pearl Township**
   > "Thomas S. Long was born in Bucks county, Pa., Oct. 3, 1807... Mr. Long is a son
   > of Morgan and Rachel Long, deceased. Mrs. Long was born Nov. 7, 1810, and is a
   > daughter of Barney and Margaret Deemer, deceased."
   **Wave-1 misreading corrected:** Rachel Deemer was NOT his mother. His mother is
   Rachel Long (maiden name not given); DEEMER is his wife's family. The German
   surname enters this family by marriage, so the Pearl Township Longs are a weaker
   "German Long" candidate than previously synthesized. Do not assert this is the
   German Long line.
   Also confirmed: Chapman prints "married in 1865 to Miss Emily, daughter of John
   Long" — the compiled bio's "daughter of Abner" is an uncited alteration of Chapman.

## The Patton conclusion, stated precisely

Chapman directly makes "Robert N." a child of John F. Long AND Sally A. Patton.
The identification of Chapman's "Robert N." with our Robert Nelson Long (m. Ann
Ghent Sleight, Pike County, 1857) is one inference resting on surname+initial,
same father, same county, and consistent chronology. Land it with the inference
explicit:

> Chapman lists "Robert N." among the eight children of John F. Long and Sally A.
> Patton; identified with Robert Nelson Long (m. Ann Ghent Sleight, 1857) on
> father, county, and chronology.
