# The 1950 households (2026-07-09)

First research thread run end-to-end through the cockpit (`gen ancestry` with the

## Homer's second family — Knob Hill, El Paso County, Colorado

1950 census (Ancestry 62308 rec 18687802): Homer Mundell, 39, dump-truck loader;
wife Leora, 30; children Jerry (8), Walter C. (6), Donald L. (4), and a daughter
(presumed living, excluded). This fixes three of the five predeceased brothers in
Evelyn's obituary — Jerry, Walt, Don — as Homer + Leora's children, i.e. Evelyn's
half-siblings; Cy and Rich were evidently born after 1950. The El Paso County
residence explains the later geography: Homer's 1956 remarriage to Clara Isbell
Walker at Colorado Springs, and Herbert's 1972 death in Colorado Springs.

## The grandmother's household, a decade on — Kingery Township, Thomas County, Kansas

1950 census (62308 rec 28350170): Albert L. Adolph, 68; Mary F. Adolph, 55; an
adult son (presumed living, excluded); **Evelyn Mundell, 15, granddaughter;
Herbert D. Mundell, 13, grandson**. Read together with the 1940 Barrett Township
record, the raising story is now documented at primary tier across BOTH ends of
the decade: Marjorie's two children stayed with their paternal grandmother from
the divorce through at least 1950, while their father raised his second family
600 miles away. Ages align exactly (Evelyn b. Jan 1935; Herbert b. Nov 1936).

Bonus resolution for case.05: "Lon A. Adolph, 58" (1940) vs "Albert L. Adolph, 68"
(1950) — same wife, same township cluster, consistent ages: one man, 'Lon' a form
of Albert L. The Albert-vs-Lon wrinkle in the case file is closed.

## The Rogers negative

Colorado marriage index (61366), bride Leora Mundell: no remarriage rows. The one
"Leora Mendell" (Denver, 1937, m. Meininger) predates the Homer–Leora marriage and
carries Mendell as an existing surname — a different woman. If Leora later became
a Rogers, as Donald Lynn Mundell's obituary wording implies, that marriage happened
outside this index — Kansas is the natural next place to look, or the full text of
Donald's obituary would settle the relationship directly.
