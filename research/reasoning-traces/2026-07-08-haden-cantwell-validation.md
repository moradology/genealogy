# Haden + Cantwell clusters: validation (2026-07-08)

## Haden (case.12)

1. **CONFIRMED (verbatim primary) — Christ Church Parish Register, Middlesex Co
   VA** (archive.org parishregisterof00chri):
   > "John Moseley & Mary Nichols Married October ye 10. 1717."
   > "Jenny daughter of John & Mary Moseley born Octo. 1. baptized October ye 14 1722."
   > "Mary Moseley dyed Jan'y ye 17. 1730."
   CAVEAT: the register proves the Moseley vitals; it does NOT marry Jenny to
   John Haden — that bridge stays COMPILED. Jenny is one of several Moseley
   daughters (Sarah, Elizabeth, John b. 1727 also in the register).

2. **CONFIRMED — King William County records loss** (Library of Virginia county
   catalog; URL moved: lva.virginia.gov/collections/ccmf/VA/VA145): "Most
   records were destroyed by fire in 1885." Citation URL needs updating to the
   new path. This is why the "John Hayden" father theory cannot currently be
   adjudicated either way.

3. **CONFIRMED — the single-compiler finding.** The colonial Anthony→John→Joseph
   Haden narrative traces to ONE compiler, Kay Haden (Birmingham AL), across
   moreleaves.blogspot.com ("Anthony Haden was my husband's ancestor"),
   sites.rootsmagic.com/KaysAncestry, and the ~katy rootsweb site (Wayback).
   Correction to wave 1: the compiler identity is Kay Haden, NOT "Angela Kay
   Mayer". Link-rot note: rootsweb.com is now entirely Cloudflare-gated — cite
   blogspot/rootsmagic or Wayback captures.

## Cantwell (case.09) — the wave-3 "resolution", graded honestly

1. **CONFIRMED (verbatim, open non-forum) — the 1888 Henry County history bio of
   Samuel Cantwell** (iagenweb.org/henry/Bios/hbiosc.htm): Thomas and Jemima
   (Kelley) Cantwell as the parentage generation ("Thomas and probably his wife
   were of Irish parentage"); ten children; the widow's remarriage to John
   Baker; Barnabas "coming first in 1846 ... removed to Adams County, Neb., and
   died there in 1883." Strongest Cantwell source on file — LAND at case.09.
   NEW: TWO Hezekiahs (Thomas's son, tailor of Coshocton, m. Mary Rannels; vs.
   Barnabas's son Hezekiah 1838-1886) — uncle and nephew; this collision
   produced the earlier sibling-cluster confusion.

2. **CONFIRMED — Cantwell surname geography** (Wikipedia): "predominantly
   concentrated in counties Tipperary, Kilkenny and Waterford" — in tension
   with the family's "Dublin" oral tradition.

   Barnabas's SON Hezekiah, re-linking the Shuler/Ruby cluster to grandchildren):
   rests entirely on Cloudflare-blocked Find a Grave memorials; no capture this
