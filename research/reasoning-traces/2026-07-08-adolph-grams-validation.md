# Adolph + Grams clusters: validation (2026-07-08)

Independent validation of the frontier-intake Adolph and Grams clusters
(`research/frontier-intake/12-adolph-winona.md`, `15-grams-rolof.md`; research/archive/program-board-2026-07-08.md 3.12,
3.15). All seven claims re-fetched and quote-matched by a validation agent on
2026-07-08 (Find a Grave reachable only through a reader proxy this session; the
1950 census read from the schedule image itself). All seven LAND-READY, with the
conflicts below carried as conflicts, never resolved silently.

## Verdicts

1. **CONFIRMED (primary, read from the census image) — 1950 census, Evelyn.**
   Kansas, Thomas County, Kingery Township, E.D. 97-9, sheet 1, household 2:
   Albert L. Adolph, head, 68, b. Kansas; Mary F., wife, 55, b. Kansas; John D.,
   son, 21; **Mundell, Evelyn — granddaughter — 15 — born Colorado.**
   Privacy rule applied: the household also contains one further minor grandchild
   (b. ~1937) who may be living; identity deliberately not recorded here.

2. **CONFIRMED — Jacob Henry Adolph's 1935 obituary** (memorial 45842484):
   > "Mr. Adolph was a son of Charles and Emmeline Adolph and was born at Indiana,
   > January 8, 1857. He was married January 16, 1875, to Miss Flora Kenyon at
   > Lyndon." ... "Those surviving are ... Lon Adolph of Colby".
   CONFLICTS on the same page: marriage 16 Jan 1875 (obit) vs. "m. 1877"
   (structured field); Flora's death 24 Oct 1921 (obit) vs. 1924 (structured).

3. **CONFIRMED — the Colby relocation clipping.** On Glenn Howard Adolph's
   memorial (242242358): "He was the son of Mr. and Mrs. Lon Adolph, August 16,
   1922, who came here from Scranton, Kansas." Cite precisely: **The Colby Free
   Press, 24 Aug 1922, p. 8** (the memorial's second clipping is The Colby
   Tribune, 25 Jan 1923, p. 1 — a different item).

4. **CONFIRMED with a SOURCE CORRECTION — Charles Adolph's 1914 obituary.**
   It is NOT on Find a Grave (his memorial 30908529 has only a compiled bio,
   birthplace "Elmo, Alsace, France"). The obituary transcription lives at
   freepages.rootsweb.com/~carriesfamily/genealogy/**adolphcharles.htm** (the
   intake mis-filed it under adolphhenry.htm):
   > "The Pomona Republican ... Thursday, January 15, 1914 ... Charles Adolph died
   > at the home of his son, Jacob Adolph, in Scranton, December 31st. He was born
   > in Germany, October 29, 1825."
   Tier: family-historian transcription of the newspaper = verified-index, not
   primary. CONFLICTS: "Germany" (obit) vs. "France / Elmo, Alsace" (memorial) —
   the classic post-1871 Alsace label conflict; birth 29 Oct (obit) vs. 29 Nov
   (memorial structured).

5. **CONFIRMED verbatim — Rosa (Rolof) Grams, memorial 48431935.** Obituary
   (The Sheridan County Democrat, Hoxie, 12 Apr 1895, p. 8 — which renders her
   name "Rosa Grim"):
   > "Died, on Saturday, March 30, 1895, at 3:03 p.m., at the residence of her
   > son-in-law John Zodrow, of Sheridan township ... aged 93 years, 1 month and
   > 22 days. Her remains were laid to rest in St. Mary cemetery on Tuesday,
   > April 2d ... was born in Germany in the year 1802; she came to America in
   > 1875 ... She leaves four daughters ... two of them living in Germany, one in
   > Wisconsin, and Mrs. Zodrow of this county. Mrs. Grim was a member of the
   > Catholic church for nearly 85 years."
   Bio adds: "Daughter of Antonius Roelofs. Married Johann Grams b. 1877, Germany."
   CONFLICTS to carry: "St. Mary cemetery" (obit) vs. "Calvary Cemetery, Leoville"
   (structured — plausibly the same Marian parish ground, unproven); "Johann Grams
   b. 1877" is chronologically impossible (contributor error; never land 1877).
   The obituary's name-form "Rosa Grim" is itself a finding for name-variant
   searches.

6. **CONFIRMED verbatim — KSGenWeb Leoville Calvary transcription:**
   "Grams | Rosa | 2/7/1802 | 3/30/1895" — independent of Find a Grave and
   internally consistent with the obituary's stated age.

7. **CONFIRMED — Antonette Grams Zodrow, memorial 156365204.** Parents field
   links ONLY Rosa (no father). Spouse John Zodrow 1843-1915 (m. 1867). Her dates
   23 May 1846 - 1 Aug 1934. Children: nine, including Julius Henry 1869-1959
   (note: memorial's 1869 vs. his death certificate's 9 Jun 1870 — the existing
   birth-year conflict), Leonard **Ferdinand** 1874-1961, Hedwig "Hattie"
   1885-1961, Anthony "Tony" 1888-1963.
   NAME-COLLISION WARNING for landing: two Leonards one generation apart —
   Leonard Ferdinand Zodrow (b. 1874, son of John + Antonette; the "Leonard,
   Selden Kansas" of Hattie's 1961 obituary) vs. Leonard William Zodrow (b. 1897,
   son of Julius + Ernestina, d. 1983 Washington). Never conflate.

## The Winona tension (case.05)

Every hard Adolph record found — the 1922 and 1923 Colby clippings, the 1935
obituary, the 1950 census in Kingery Township (Thomas County) — points to
Thomas/Osage County. The "Winona, Logan County" schooling claim still rests
solely on Evelyn's own obituary wording, and Winona Cemetery holds zero
family burials (validated negative). Carry in case.05 as an unresolved tension.
