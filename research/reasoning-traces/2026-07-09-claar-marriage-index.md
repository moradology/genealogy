# Claar Marriage Place Fixed, Bride Surname Still Open

## Question

Was Samuel Dove Claar's 1895 marriage in Iowa or Kansas, and which maiden
spelling belongs to his bride?

## Evidence Read

Decatur County's groom index lists Samuel D. Claar marrying Maggie M. Hogle on
5 October 1895 at Oberlin. The bride-sorted page repeats the same entry. These
are two views of one volunteer transcription, not two independent records, and
the site warns that some names and dates may be imperfect.

FamilySearch's Kansas marriage-image collection omits Decatur County. That
coverage gap explains why the earlier subscription search failed to find the
marriage; the failure never supported an Iowa location. Other derivative
records render the surname Hoyl, Hoyle, and Hayle.

## Conclusion

The date and Oberlin, Kansas, location are strong at indexed tier. The bride's
exact maiden spelling remains open, so no parent line should be attached yet.
case.17 now asks only for the original record and surname adjudication.

## Next Moves

Request the original marriage license or register entry from Decatur County
District Court and inspect the bride's spelling, residence, witnesses, and any
parent information.
