## Tier 1 — order now (highest payoff)

### 1. The 1939 Homer–Marjorie divorce case file  ★ best single record
- WHERE: Colorado State Archives (denver; archives.colorado.gov, 1313 Sherman St, Denver
  CO 80203), which holds statewide divorce records; if not there, the District Court of the
  county of filing. Filing county unknown — likely Washington, Kit Carson, Logan, or
  Yuma Co (their eastern-CO home). The Colorado State Archives 1890-1939 statewide divorce
  index will give the case number and county; request the full case file (petition + decree),
  not just the index line.
- COST: nominal search + per-page copy fee (~$10-30).
- PROVES / ADVANCES: divorce petitions routinely name the minor children (Evelyn, Herbert —
  corroborating the grandmother-raising), state residences, and sometimes the wife's maiden
  particulars; critically, the decree may show where Marjorie went / her post-divorce name,
  which is the thread to her later life and eventually her death certificate (which WOULD
  name her parents). This is the record most likely to break the case open.

### 2. Evelyn Delores Mundell's Colorado birth certificate
- WHERE: Colorado Dept. of Public Health & Environment (CDPHE) Vital Records
  (cdphe.colorado.gov/vital-records), or Kit Carson County. Event: 16 Jan 1935, Flagler.
- COST: ~$20.
- PROVES / ADVANCES: names the mother as Marjorie Evelyn Clemans and gives her stated
  AGE and BIRTHPLACE (and often the parents' birthplaces). Marjorie's birthplace would
  narrow the search for her own family to a specific state/county — the missing anchor that
  made the online census search impossible. Cheapest way to get a new, hard lead.
